01 The Opportunity

West Africa sits at an inflection point in its energy transition. Generation capacity is growing — yet the infrastructure to convert that capacity into commercial revenue remains broken. The opportunity is not to build more power; it is to capture the value from what is already being generated.

Despite a 57.7% electrification rate across a population of 400 million, the region's utilities are trapped in a structural paradox: the more electricity they produce, the larger their unrecovered losses become. GridIQ breaks this cycle by transforming invisible loss into measurable, recoverable revenue — and by turning siloed utility data into a live, credible signal for DFIs, regulators, and investors.

The core market thesis: Revenue recovery from existing generation is faster, cheaper, and less risky than adding new generation capacity. A utility recovering just 5 percentage points of NTL recaptures millions of dollars annually — without a single new megawatt installed.
02 The Problem Being Solved

Two crises compound each other across the ECOWAS grid: runaway non-technical losses and near-total energy data blindness. Neither can be solved without the other.

The ECOWAS EIS Data Blindness Gap

Even where losses are partially understood at utility level, this knowledge does not reach ECOWAS regulators, DFIs, or national planning ministries. The ECOWAS Energy Information System relies on voluntary, manual reporting, with lags of 2–8 years across most member states. DFIs conducting due diligence cannot obtain current, verified utility performance data — forcing higher risk premiums and, in some cases, declining to invest altogether.

04 The AI Opportunity Engine — Core Differentiator

GridIQ's competitive core is its AI intelligence layer — the set of machine learning capabilities that transform raw sensor and meter data into actionable commercial intelligence. Four distinct AI functions work in concert.

Token-stream NTL detection
The only NTL engine in the region calibrated for prepaid token-purchase event streams — the dominant metering paradigm across ECOWAS. Detects meter bypass, illegal reconnection, and billing manipulation without requiring monthly cycle data.
Georeferenced loss attribution
Compares injected power against metered consumption at feeder and transformer level. AI engine isolates loss zones in real time and generates georeferenced alerts identifying specific clusters for field investigation.
Predictive maintenance ranking
Continuous monitoring of voltage deviation, power factor, load factor, and phase asymmetry across all instrumented transformers. Monthly maintenance priority rankings sent to engineering teams — estimated to reduce unplanned outages by 15–25% by Year 2.
Revenue recovery quantification
Real-time calculation of recovered revenue attributable to platform interventions: (Baseline NTL kWh − Current NTL kWh) × utility tariff. Cumulative monthly tracker with DFI-formatted export — creating an auditable accountability record for donors and lenders.
What makes this hard to replicate: The AI models are trained on the specific token-purchase event structures of Conlog and Hexing prepaid systems — the two dominant meter vendors in the ECOWAS market. Adapting generic anomaly detection to this paradigm requires proprietary training data and deep utility integration that cannot be acquired quickly.

07 Competitive Landscape & Moat

GridIQ competes in a market where the main alternatives are expensive Western smart grid platforms built for fully-metered, digitised utilities — a profile that describes almost no ECOWAS utility today. Generic platforms require deep AMI infrastructure that does not exist; they are also not built for the token-purchase metering paradigm. GridIQ's moat is structural, not just technological.

Prepaid token-stream AI The only NTL detection engine adapted to Conlog/Hexing token-purchase event streams — the dominant meter paradigm across ECOWAS. Competitors require monthly AMI data that most utilities do not have.
ECOWAS EIS integration GridIQ is the only platform with a direct, standardised API connection to the ECOWAS Energy Information System — making it the path of least resistance for any ECOWAS member utility seeking to meet reporting obligations.
Context-specific hardware stack IP65 ruggedised sensors with LoRaWAN fallback and 48-hour battery backup are designed specifically for West African substation environments — not repurposed from European or North American deployments.
DFI reporting as a native feature The Impact ROI Tracker generates grant accountability reports in AfDB, IFC, AFD, and EU formats as a built-in platform function — a capability no other grid analytics platform has engineered for this market.
Low-bandwidth, bilingual design The platform is 2G/3G-optimised with a full English/French interface — two design constraints that eliminate all major Western competitors from realistic deployment consideration.
08 Go-to-Market Strategy

GridIQ enters the market through a pilot-anchor strategy, using a single high-visibility utility deployment to generate verified loss reduction data, DFI relationships, and regional credibility — then scaling across the ECOWAS corridor on the strength of audited outcomes.

Phase 1 · Yr 1
Pilot Deployment — EDSA (Sierra Leone) Single utility pilot zone. 10 IoT sensors deployed. Baseline NTL measurement established. AI model calibrated to utility's Hexing/Conlog token data. First live EIS report generated. Recovery ROI documented for DFI reporting.
Phase 2 · Yr 2
Expansion — ECG & NEDCO (Ghana) Leverage Sierra Leone outcomes data to onboard Ghana's two distribution utilities. Expand sensor network. Refine AI models on a second national metering dataset. Scale DFI grant pipeline through AfDB and EU Energy Access programmes.
Phase 3 · Yr 3–4
ECOWAS Regional Rollout Target NAWEC (Gambia), SBEE (Benin), SENELEC (Senegal). Position GridIQ as the de facto ECOWAS utility intelligence standard. Monetise regional EIS data feed as standalone subscription product for ECOWAS Secretariat and DFIs.
Phase 4 · Yr 5+
Continental Platform Licensing License platform and AI models to utilities in East and Southern Africa (EAPP/SAPP corridors). Partner with major DFIs to embed GridIQ as a standard component of energy access financing covenants.
DFI as channel, not just funder: By building DFI-format reporting natively into the platform, GridIQ makes itself a procurement preference for any AfDB, IFC, or EU-funded utility modernisation programme. Each grant-funded deployment is also a sales case study for the next.

P0

Government & Political Risk

The elephant in the room — sovereign non-payment, regulatory capture, and political interference

Impact

Key Risks

  • • Sovereign non-payment from insolvent utilities
  • • Political interference with revenue protection
  • • Regulatory capture protecting utilities
  • • Leadership changes every 2–4 years

Blind Spots

  • • Assumes utilities can pay SaaS fees
  • • No political heat map feature
  • • No escalation protocol for sensitive alerts
  • • No institutional memory strategy

Recommended Fixes

  • → Engage only utilities with active DFI reform programs
  • → Draw fees from DFI technical assistance, not utility opex
  • → Route politically sensitive alerts through legal/compliance
  • → Train permanent ministry staff, not just management

Critical Insight: EDSA (Sierra Leone) and EDG (Guinea) are insolvent. They cannot pay vendors, let alone SaaS fees. The document assumes payment capability that does not exist.

P0

The "Who Pays?" Problem

Revenue model fiction — insolvent utilities cannot afford $3K–40K/month SaaS

Impact

Current Model Flaws

Utility pays SaaS✗ Broken

Utility has no money — loses 25-40% to theft, cannot collect from paying customers

Hardware sold to utility✗ Broken

Utility has no capex budget, already in debt to IPPs and DFIs

Revenue share on recovery✗ Broken

Utility cannot attribute causality; disputes inevitable

Restructured Model

Primary: DFI pays✓ Viable

AfDB, World Bank, EU/AFD fund as technical assistance. They need verified impact data.

Secondary: Ministry pays✓ Viable

National energy ministries need EIS data for policy and DFI compact conditions.

Tertiary: Utility gets free✓ Viable

Platform bundled in DFI reform packages. Utility commits to data sharing only.

P1

Cybersecurity & Data Sovereignty

Dangerously absent — cloud-hosted SCADA data with no security framework in 2026

Impact
Data Residency

Nigeria, Ghana require citizen data stored locally. Commit to in-country AWS/Azure regions.

Air-Gapped Option

High-security utilities may refuse cloud. Offer on-premise deployment for Enterprise tier.

SOC 2 / ISO 27001

DFI procurement increasingly requires certifications. Roadmap needed; interim pentest reports.

IoT Security

Unsecured sensors = grid attack vectors. Secure boot, signed firmware, encrypted LoRaWAN.

Alert: 2023–2024 saw multiple ransomware attacks on South African and Nigerian utilities. Nigeria's NCC, Ghana's NCA, and ECOWAS are drafting critical infrastructure cybersecurity frameworks.

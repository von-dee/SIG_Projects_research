# Skill Monetization & Consulting

## The Strategy
Turn expertise into income by selling specialized knowledge rather than just labor. This is the fastest path to high income for individuals with rare, valuable skills.

## Why It Works
- **Scarcity Premium**: Deep expertise in niche fields commands 5-10x standard hourly rates
- **Global Access**: Platforms eliminate geographic limitations on client base
- **Low Capital Requirements**: No inventory, infrastructure, or team needed to start
- **Immediate Cash Flow**: Billable hours convert directly to revenue

## Company Example: **McKinsey & Company** (Non-Mining Sector)

### Business Model
McKinsey operates the world's most prestigious management consulting firm, monetizing elite problem-solving expertise at massive scale.

**Revenue Mechanics:**
- **Engagement Fees**: $500K–$50M+ per project depending on scope and duration
- **Partner-Leveraged Model**: Partners ($3M–$10M/year) sell work; junior consultants ($80K–$200K) deliver it
- **Knowledge Capitalization**: Insights from one engagement are codified, repackaged, and resold across industries

**The Arbitrage:**
McKinsey buys young talent (top MBA graduates) at $150K–$200K/year and resells their output at $10,000–$25,000 per consultant per day to Fortune 500 clients. The 50x markup reflects brand trust, methodology, and access—not just raw labor.

**Key Insight:** McKinsey doesn't sell *time*—it sells *certainty*. CEOs pay premium rates not for the hours worked, but for the reduced risk of making billion-dollar decisions.

## How to Apply
1. **Identify Your 1% Skill**: What do you know that 99% of the market cannot do?
2. **Productize Knowledge**: Turn services into repeatable frameworks (templates, playbooks, courses)
3. **Platform Selection**: Toptal for tech, GLG for expert networks, direct LinkedIn for B2B
4. **Price for Value, Not Time**: Anchor to outcomes ($50K revenue increase) not hours (40 hours × $200)

## Risk Factors
- **Income Ceiling**: Trading time for money has hard limits (168 hours/week)
- **Client Concentration**: One client leaving can collapse revenue
- **Skill Depreciation**: Expertise must be continuously updated

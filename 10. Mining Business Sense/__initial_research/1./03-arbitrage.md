# Arbitrage

## The Strategy
Profit from pricing inefficiencies by buying undervalued assets in one market and selling them in another where they command higher value. The purest form of "buy low, sell high."

## Why It Works
- **Market Friction**: Information asymmetry and geographic separation create persistent gaps
- **Speed Advantage**: Acting on insights before the crowd captures the spread
- **Low Risk (Theoretically)**: Simultaneous buy/sell locks in profit regardless of market direction
- **Infinite Variations**: Geographic, temporal, informational, regulatory, and product arbitrage

## Company Example: **Glencore plc** (Mining Sector)

### Business Model
Glencore is the world's largest commodity trader, built entirely on sophisticated arbitrage across mining, metals, and energy markets.

**Revenue Mechanics:**
- **Physical Arbitrage**: Buys copper concentrate from mines in Zambia at $7,000/ton, processes in Switzerland, sells to Chinese manufacturers at $8,500/ton
- **Geographic Arbitrage**: Exploits price differences between London Metal Exchange (LME) and Shanghai Futures Exchange (SHFE)
- **Temporal Arbitrage**: Stores oil when prices are low ($60/bbl), sells when seasonal demand spikes ($85/bbl)
- **Logistics Arbitrage**: Owns 200+ vessels, 30+ ports, and 50+ storage facilities to physically move commodities to highest-value markets

**The Arbitrage Engine:**
```
Zambia Copper Mine → Glencore Trading Desk → Chinese Smelter
        $6,500/ton          +$500 logistics      = $8,500 sale
        + $1,500 refining margin
        = $2,500 gross profit per ton
```

**Key Insight:** Glencore doesn't *produce* most commodities—it *optimizes their location in time and space*. The company employs 1,000+ traders who exist solely to find and exploit price discrepancies before competitors. Information is the inventory; speed is the moat.

## How to Apply
1. **Map the Spread**: Identify two markets with persistent price differences for identical goods
2. **Account for Friction**: Shipping, taxes, currency risk, and time decay erode margins
3. **Build the Bridge**: Create logistics, relationships, or information systems to execute the trade
4. **Scale the Repetition**: Arbitrage compresses over time; volume and speed are the defenses

**Accessible Forms:**
- **Retail Arbitrage**: Buy clearance items at Walmart, resell on Amazon FBA
- **Domain Flipping**: Purchase expired domains with SEO value, resell to businesses
- **Crypto Arbitrage**: Exploit price differences across exchanges (diminishing)
- **Labor Arbitrage**: Hire talent in low-cost countries, sell services in high-cost markets

## Risk Factors
- **Execution Risk**: Prices move during transaction; hedging costs eat margins
- **Regulatory Risk**: Cross-border trade faces tariffs, sanctions, and capital controls
- **Competition**: Spreads attract competitors and eventually compress to zero

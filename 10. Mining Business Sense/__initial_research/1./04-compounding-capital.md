# Compounding Capital

## The Strategy
Reinvest returns so that gains generate further gains. Time becomes the primary wealth engine—not labor, not luck, not genius. Albert Einstein allegedly called compound interest the "eighth wonder of the world."

## Why It Works
- **Exponential Curve**: 15% annual returns turn $100K into $813K in 15 years, $1.6M in 20 years
- **Passive Acceleration**: After year 10, returns exceed original principal contributions
- **Mathematical Certainty**: Given positive real returns and sufficient time, compounding is inevitable
- **Behavioral Edge**: Requires patience, which most market participants lack

## Company Example: **Berkshire Hathaway** (Non-Mining Sector)

### Business Model
Warren Buffett's conglomerate is the most successful demonstration of compounding capital in history—$19 per share in 1965 to $600,000+ in 2024.

**Revenue Mechanics:**
- **Insurance Float**: Collect premiums today, pay claims years later. The "float" ($168B) is invested for Berkshire's benefit, not shareholders'—an interest-free loan from time
- **Reinvestment Discipline**: 99% of earnings retained and redeployed since 1965
- **Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR)**: 19.8% over 59 years vs. 10.4% for S&P 500
- **The Snowball**: $1,000 invested in 1965 = $36 million today

**The Compounding Architecture:**
| Decade | Starting Value | Ending Value | Primary Drivers |
|--------|---------------|--------------|-----------------|
| 1965–1975 | $19/share | $94/share | Textile mills, insurance acquisitions |
| 1975–1985 | $94 | $2,400 | GEICO, Washington Post, See's Candies |
| 1985–1995 | $2,400 | $25,000 | Coca-Cola, Gillette, Capital Cities/ABC |
| 1995–2005 | $25,000 | $88,000 | MidAmerican Energy, reinsurance |
| 2005–2015 | $88,000 | $200,000 | BNSF Railway, Apple stake |
| 2015–2024 | $200,000 | $600,000+ | Apple, energy, Japanese trading houses |

**Key Insight:** Berkshire doesn't *trade*—it *owns*. Buffett's edge isn't stock-picking skill but structural patience. By refusing to sell and continuously reinvesting cash flows, he transformed a failing textile mill into the world's 8th largest company. The "secret" is publicly available; almost no one can execute it.

## How to Apply
1. **Start Early**: $500/month at 8% from age 25 = $1.7M at 65. Starting at 35 = $745K.
2. **Minimize Friction**: Tax-advantaged accounts (401k, IRA, ISA) preserve compounding
3. **Avoid Interruption**: Every withdrawal resets the exponential curve
4. **Select Vehicles**:
   - **Index Funds**: VOO, VTI (low cost, broad diversification)
   - **Dividend Growth**: Companies with 25+ years of increasing payouts
   - **Private Business**: Reinvest profits rather than extracting lifestyle income

## Risk Factors
- **Sequence Risk**: Negative returns early in retirement can devastate portfolios
- **Inflation Erosion**: Nominal returns must exceed real inflation to compound wealth
- **Behavioral Risk**: Panic selling during downturns destroys decades of progress

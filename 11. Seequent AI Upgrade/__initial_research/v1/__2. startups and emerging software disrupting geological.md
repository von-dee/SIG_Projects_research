Here's a comprehensive look at the startups and emerging software disrupting geological modeling in mining:

---

## 🤖 AI-Powered Mineral Discovery

**KoBold Metals** (USA, founded 2018) is arguably the highest-profile disruptor. Their AI technology aggregates data from satellite imagery and historical drilling reports to map the Earth's crust in detail, pinpointing untapped resources that traditional methods might miss. By 2025, KoBold had raised nearly $1 billion, backed by Breakthrough Energy Ventures, Andreessen Horowitz, and BHP Ventures, reaching a valuation of $2.96 billion.

**SensOre** (Australia) takes a similar approach — BHP made a venture investment in SensOre for AI-driven drilling target identification, which contributed to unlocking $1 billion in value from data automation.

**Minerva Intelligence** (Canada) — provides an AI platform called TERRA for mineral exploration, combining AI, geological models, and exploratory data to optimize exploration efforts by identifying potential mineral deposits.

---

## 🌈 Hyperspectral & Remote Sensing

**Plotlogic** (Australia) is one of the most exciting hardware-software plays. Their OreSense® technology uses advanced sensors and proprietary AI processing to deliver near real-time, high-resolution material classification that supports faster decision-making across all mining stages. It can scan all rocks in a mine — surface or underground — and deliver decision-ready data in just 15 minutes, compared to over an hour with traditional methods. Customers include BHP, Vale, and South32.

**Matter Intelligence & TerraEye** — these platforms map surface mineralogy remotely using hyperspectral satellites and drones, even under vegetation or shallow cover, revealing new targets in places inaccessible to traditional ground crews.

**Terraspec (Malvern Panalytical)** — provides portable spectrometers that offer rapid, non-destructive analysis of mineral compositions on-site using near-infrared spectroscopy, bringing lab-quality analysis directly to the field.

---

## 🖼️ Core Logging & Image Analysis

**Imago (acquired by Seequent)** — offers a cloud-based platform for centralized storage and AI-enhanced analysis of geological imagery, enabling shared annotations and insights among geologists across different locations. Their AutoCrop tool uses machine learning to automatically crop and linearize imagery of core samples — a process that has transformed from hours to minutes.

**Datarock** (Australia) — provides premium AI-supported geological workflow modeling, focused on automating the interpretation of drill core and rock face images.

**FastGeo** — distinguishes itself by integrating high-performance image visualization with geological logging and advanced AI services, accelerating core logging workflows significantly.

---

## 🛰️ Quantum & Next-Gen Sensing

**Atomionics** (Singapore) — develops advanced quantum gravimeters and magnetometers that detect subtle variations in subsurface density and magnetic properties, even through complex geology, expanding the frontier of what's discoverable underground.

---

## 🌐 Geospatial Data Platforms

**Genereso** (Switzerland) — offers a SaaS platform with geospatial data for mining industries in Latin America, combining mining, cadastral, geological, and environmental data into web maps, synchronizing real-time data from governmental portals for up-to-date information on exploration projects.

---

## The Big Picture

Startups are entering areas such as satellite-based tailings monitoring, AI geology modeling, and low-cost IoT retrofits — with the focus in 2025 on integration, so that planning, plant, fleet, and ESG reporting all point to the same data source.

The key disruption is **speed and precision** — where traditional modeling took weeks of manual interpretation, these tools are compressing that to hours or minutes with far less human error. The biggest challenge remains data: mining data is often fragmented across decades, companies, and formats, and control of proprietary datasets can create a significant competitive advantage.

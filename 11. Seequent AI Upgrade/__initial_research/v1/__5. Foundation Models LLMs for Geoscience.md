Here's the full breakdown mapped to each disrupting technology:

## 1. 🏗️ Foundation Models (LLMs for Geoscience)

**GeoGPT** — publicly released in April 2025, it is a domain-specific foundation model tailored for geoscientists, providing multimodal Q&A capabilities that allow users to upload geological maps and retrieve information through interactive dialogues, extracting latitude/longitude coordinates and legend information.

**RadiXplore** — built specifically for mining and exploration teams to search, analyze, and extract insights from historical reports, trained on millions of geological documents including scanned and handwritten formats, enabling geologists to find trends, pinpoint anomalies, or identify overlooked targets in seconds. The UI flow is: connect archive → type natural language query → view ranked results with highlighted evidence → clip to target generation workflow.

---

## 2. 🌊 Generative / Diffusion Models

This is the least commercially deployed technology right now — it lives mostly in research but is moving fast. The closest production implementations are **conditional simulation engines** inside platforms like **RMSP (GeologicAI)** and **Seequent Evo**, which generate probabilistic subsurface realizations from sparse drill data. 3D latent diffusion models are now being developed for parameterization of facies-based geological systems, enabling history matching with variable geological metaparameters — a significant extension over 2D fixed-parameter systems. The first commercial tools embedding this will likely emerge from the oil & gas sector (where CoNFiLD-geo is being tested) before crossing into hard rock mining.

---

## 3. 🧠 Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs)

Physics-Integrated Neural Networks are now being applied to accurate estimation of mineralogy from geophysical well logs, combining data-driven learning with domain-specific physical constraints and embedding petrophysical relationships directly into the neural network architecture — ensuring predictions adhere to physical laws.

The closest deployed products are **RMSP's conditional simulation** (enforces geostatistical physical constraints) and **Maptek Vestrex / DomainMCF** which uses physically-constrained RBF interpolants. Maptek's GeologyCore 2025 introduced a global RBF engine capable of modeling complex geology — veins, faults, and intrusions — across entire regions and datasets, with new support for Linear and Spheroidal RBF interpolants for greater geological realism. Pure PINN deployments in mining software are still 12–24 months from commercial release.

---

## 4. 🕸️ Graph Neural Networks

GNNs are currently embedded inside **KoBold Metals'** proprietary platform and **SensOre**, where they model spatial relationships between geochemical, geophysical, and geological signals across large terranes. No standalone commercial GNN geological modeling tool exists yet — this is the biggest white space in the market.

---

## 5. 🤖 Semi-supervised & Transfer Learning

This is the most widely deployed AI category right now. Every platform in the widget uses it:

- Seequent's Driver uses machine learning to quickly understand the geological structure of deposits and improve downstream models with little manual effort and no coding, automatically informing 3D models while still allowing geologists to adjust based on their own understanding of the ore body.
- Datarock uses a "geologist-in-the-loop" design that speeds up core logging, creates a repeatable standard, and frees up geologists to focus on insights rather than box-by-box grunt work, with strategic integrations with IMDEX and ELEMISSION for end-to-end workflows.

---

## 6. 🔍 Explainable AI (XAI)

Datarock explicitly markets "production-ready, explainable AI that standardises characterisation, accelerates updates, and integrates with your existing software and databases" — the explainability is a direct selling point for JORC/NI 43-101 compliance. **GeologicAI's RMSP** also surfaces uncertainty per block, which functions as a form of spatial explainability for resource estimates.

---

## 7. 🛰️ Multimodal AI

**GeologicAI** is the most complete implementation: its mobile trailer-based scanning units are equipped with five sensor types — hyperspectral (SWIR/VNIR), XRF, high-resolution RGB photography, LiDAR, and magnetic susceptibility — with all data stitched together at the pixel level and analyzed by proprietary AI models. Through its acquisition of Lumo Analytics, GeologicAI now offers the only truly comprehensive and integrated source of sensor data for the critical minerals industry, adding LIBS-based drill core analysis for rare-earth and light element detection.

## The Gap Map

| Technology | Maturity in Products | Biggest Gap |
|---|---|---|
| Semi-supervised / Transfer learning | ✅ Widely deployed | Needs more cross-geology transfer |
| Multimodal AI | ✅ GeologicAI, Plotlogic, Datarock | No open API standard across sensors |
| Explainable AI | ✅ Datarock, RMSP | Not yet in major platforms (Surpac, Vulcan) |
| Foundation LLMs | 🔶 GeoGPT (research), RadiXplore | No tool that reasons over 3D geometry |
| Generative / Diffusion | 🔶 Conditional simulation (RMSP, Evo) | True diffusion-based 3D generation not yet productized |
| Physics-Informed NNs | 🔶 RBF/implicit as proxy | No true PINN commercial tool for hard rock mining |
| Graph Neural Networks | 🔴 KoBold/SensOre (proprietary only) | Biggest white space — no standalone commercial product |

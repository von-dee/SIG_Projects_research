 Here are the most valuable additions you could make to this research repository, organized by category:

---

## 🔬 Technical Deep-Dives

**Computational benchmarks** — Real GPU memory, training times, and inference latencies for each technology at production scale. Right now the numbers are mostly theoretical or from papers.

**Failure modes and edge cases** — When do PINNs violate physics? When do diffusion models generate geologically impossible structures? When do GNNs miss deposits? Document the failure modes, not just the success stories.

**Data requirements** — How much data does each technology actually need? "Pre-train on 10,000 synthetic models" is vague. Specify: voxel dimensions, number of drill holes, seismic trace density, satellite scene count.

**Hyperparameter sensitivity** — Which knobs actually matter? PINN loss weighting (lambda_physics), diffusion guidance scale, GNN attention heads. Give ranges that work vs. ranges that break.

---

## 🏭 Industry & Market

**Pricing intelligence** — What do existing platforms actually charge? Leapfrog subscriptions, Maptek licenses, VRIFY pricing tiers, GeologicAI scanning rates. Real numbers, not estimates.

**Customer interviews** — What do working geologists actually want? What's their current pain point priority stack? What would make them switch tools? What would make them refuse AI entirely?

**Regulatory precedent** — Has any JORC or NI 43-101 report actually been accepted with AI-assisted components? What was the competent person's statement? What did the regulator question?

**Competitive response scenarios** — If Seequent announced full AI integration tomorrow, what's their likely architecture? How would Maptek respond? What's their patent portfolio?

## 💰 Business & Strategy

**Detailed financial model** — Not just TAM/SAM. Unit economics: customer acquisition cost, lifetime value, churn by segment, sales cycle length, professional services margin.

**Partnership landscape** — Who would you partner with vs. build vs. buy? Cloud providers (AWS/Azure/GCP), GPU vendors (NVIDIA), data providers (Sentinel Hub, Planet), legacy software vendors (Seequent API access).

**Talent map** — Who are the 20 people in the world who could build this? Where are they? (Zhejiang Lab GeoGPT team, KADM authors, KDCGAT researchers, ex-BHP AI team, ex-Rio Tinto RTVis engineers)

**Technical debt assessment** — If you started building today with PyTorch, where would you be locked in? What's the migration path if diffusion models are superseded by flow matching? If transformers are superseded by state space models?

---

## 🧪 Validation & Evidence

**Reproducible benchmarks** — Pick one open dataset (e.g., WAMEX Western Australia exploration data, or NSGD Nevada geothermal data). Run each technology on it. Document actual performance, not paper claims.

**Blind test protocol** — Design a protocol where geologists evaluate AI-generated models without knowing they're AI. Measure trust, actionability, and perceived quality vs. human-generated models.

**Economic validation** — For one real deposit, calculate the actual dollar impact: "If we had this platform for the [X] project, we would have saved $Y in drilling costs and discovered the ore body Z months earlier."

---

## 🌍 Domain-Specific Extensions

**Commodity-specific models** — Porphyry copper, IOCG, orogenic gold, VMS, sediment-hosted copper, lithium pegmatites, rare earth element carbonatites. Each has different geological controls, data types, and modeling priorities. A generic platform is less valuable than a commodity-specialized one.

**Jurisdiction-specific regulations** — JORC (Australia), NI 43-101 (Canada), SAMREC (South Africa), PERC (Europe), CBRR (China). Each has different requirements for data quality, competent person qualifications, and reporting standards. Document the differences.

**Climate and ESG integration** — Carbon sequestration site characterization, critical mineral supply chain mapping, tailings dam monitoring, mine closure planning. These are growing markets with regulatory tailwinds.

---

## 🔗 Integration & Ecosystem

**API specifications** — What would the actual REST API look like? Endpoint definitions, request/response schemas, authentication, rate limiting. This turns architecture into implementable specification.

**Data lineage and versioning** — How do you track that Model v3.2 was trained on Dataset v2.1 which included Drill Program 2024-Q3? This is critical for regulatory compliance but rarely documented.

**MLOps for geology** — How do you retrain models when new drill data arrives? How do you A/B test model versions? How do you monitor for concept drift in geological settings? The MLOps literature is mostly for consumer tech; geological MLOps has unique challenges (sparse data, expensive labels, non-stationary distributions).

## 📊 Visual & Interactive

**Interactive technology matrix** — A filterable table where you can select "I have sparse drill data + satellite imagery + need JORC compliance" and it tells you which technology stack to use.

**Decision tree flowchart** — "Start here: What is your data situation?" → branches to recommended AI stack.

**Comparison tables** — Side-by-side feature comparison of all existing platforms, not just the maturity matrix. Price, data formats, AI capabilities, export options, cloud vs. on-premise, support quality.

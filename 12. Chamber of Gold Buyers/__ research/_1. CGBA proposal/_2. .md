Good news — there's substantial real-world precedent for almost everything I outlined. Here's the evidence, organized by category.

## 1. A direct competitor already exists in this exact market

**GoldChain Ghana** is already selling a blockchain traceability platform specifically built for this situation. It records licensed small-scale miners' output on the blockchain to close black-market routes and support Act 1140's formalization mandate. Its pitch deck claims it can eliminate an estimated $2 billion in annual smuggling losses through immutable blockchain records, help gold meet OECD and LBMA responsible-sourcing standards to access premium ethical gold markets, and give small-scale miners verified provenance for direct market access instead of discounted middleman sales. Technically it uses GPS trackers and IoT sensors on transport vehicles feeding data to the blockchain, with smart contracts flagging route deviations or tampered seals, plus mobile and web apps for miners, aggregators, and officials to log production.

This is important evidence in two directions: it validates the market need, but it also means you're not entering a vacuum — the Chamber and GoldBod may already be evaluating or piloting this vendor.

## 2. GoldBod itself has publicly committed to building this — with a stated (missed) deadline

At the June 2025 Mining in Motion Summit, GoldBod's CEO stated the board would roll out a digital traceability technology for its entire supply chain before the end of 2025, aiming for a system where every gram of gold purchased is traceable through all custody points back to a licensed, legally compliant mine. It's now July 2026 — worth finding out via the Chamber whether that rollout happened, stalled, or was awarded to someone (possibly GoldChain). That gap is your opening line into a conversation with the Chamber.

GoldBod also already runs the regulatory infrastructure you'd need to integrate with:
- An **online licensing portal** launched April 2025, covering Buyer (Tier 1), Buyer (Tier 2), Aggregator, and Self-Financing Aggregator licenses, with refining, smelting, fabrication, storage, transportation, and importation licenses added from July 2025.
- A public **License Registry** — the official source for verifying certified licenses, covering aggregators, buyers, smelters, refiners, transporters, and jewelry fabricators. This is exactly the endpoint a "check buyer license status before transacting" feature in your POS system would query.

## 3. Global precedent for ASM gold traceability (proof the model works elsewhere)

- **DRC**: Minespider partnered with Society Artisanal to implement blockchain traceability and Digital Product Passports proving artisanal gold from the DRC is conflict-free and meets international standards, with the first blockchain-traced shipment exported in June 2024 and plans to extend traceability beyond the refinery stage.
- **Ghana (pre-GoldBod)**: Solidaridad built a chain-of-custody system for small-scale mines tracking gold and gold ore from excavation through separation, in response to growing international demand for supply-chain due diligence.
- **Peru**: a government-run registry (RECPO) lets registered gold traders and processors do online registration of sales and processing while tracking the product's destination — a close analog to a national buyer-registry-plus-transaction-log system.
- **Liberia**: an EU-backed project (Raw Materials Radar Consortium) is building a combined blockchain and IoT hardware/software solution to trace origin, ownership, and provenance of artisanal minerals.
- **Rwanda**: Cisco ran field research with miners and government/NGO stakeholders to prototype blockchain traceability, publishing it as a public whitepaper on using blockchain as a mineral-supply-chain due-diligence tool.

Takeaway: donor and development-finance money is actively flowing into exactly this category of software, which supports the NGO/development-partnership angle.

## 4. Precious-metals buyer transaction/compliance software — mature market in the US

This validates the "gold buyer POS + compliance" product category, even though it's structured differently there (law-enforcement stolen-goods reporting rather than smuggling/export control):
- **LeadsOnline** is the dominant player — an automated system receiving transaction data from pawn shops, secondhand stores, scrap metal processors, and precious metals dealers, used by police departments nationally to identify suspects and recover stolen property. One officer reported roughly 90% of cases resulting in conviction based on LeadsOnline-sourced information.
- Dedicated POS platforms (**Bravo Pawn Systems**, **PawnMaster**) are built specifically for this vertical, and current best-practice stacks pair a live metals-pricing feed wired directly into the buy/loan screen so every offer reflects current spot price, since hand-keyed or weekly-updated pricing loses money fast when metals move. That's a direct precedent for the spot-price integration I proposed.
- Regulatory pattern precedent: dealers are commonly required to keep written or electronic records of every purchase, including buyer ID type and number, kept for several years — structurally similar to the KYC/photo/ID-capture feature in the transaction system I proposed for you, just serving smuggling-prevention instead of stolen-goods-recovery.

## 5. Mobile money infrastructure for the financing/payments module

This is a live, well-capitalized rail, not a hypothetical: MTN MoMo earned $549 million in revenue from Ghana in 2025 and was recently spun off into a standalone fintech entity, MobileMoney Fintech Limited, effective March 31, 2026, with mobile money revenue up 35.7% year-on-year and advanced services like digital payments and lending up 55.9%. Ghana also has five other MoMo-style providers (AirtelTigo Cash, Vodafone Cash, Zeepay, G-Money, PalmPay), so a payments module built against MTN's API is extendable to the rest of the market later.


That actually changes the strategy significantly.

If **you're an IT company** partnering with the **Ghana Chamber of Gold Buyers** (rather than being the marketplace operator), then **you should not make your revenue dependent on commissions from gold sales**. The Chamber owns the relationship with its members; you own and operate the technology.

## Recommended Business Model: B2B SaaS + Managed Services

Position yourselves as the **technology partner** that builds and operates the Chamber's digital trading platform.

### Revenue Model

| Revenue Stream           | Who Pays          | Frequency      |
| ------------------------ | ----------------- | -------------- |
| Platform License         | Chamber           | Annual         |
| Implementation           | Chamber           | One-time       |
| Support & Maintenance    | Chamber           | Monthly/Annual |
| Custom Development       | Chamber           | As needed      |
| Member Premium Features  | Buyers            | Optional       |
| API Integrations         | Banks, Refineries | Annual         |
| Training & Certification | Chamber Members   | Per session    |

This provides predictable, recurring revenue without relying on transaction volumes.

## Leave the commercial model to the Chamber

The Chamber can decide whether to charge:

* Membership fees
* Transaction fees
* RFQ fees
* Featured listings
* Advertising
* Export service fees

Your company simply provides the software that enables those services.

Instead of saying:

This positions you as a long-term technology partner rather than a market intermediary.

## Suggested commercial proposal

For a national industry association, you could structure it as follows:

* **Platform Development:** US$150,000–300,000 (one-time)
* **Annual Platform License:** US$30,000–60,000
* **Support & Maintenance:** 15–20% of the development cost per year
* **Enhancements:** Time-and-materials or fixed-price projects
* **Cloud Hosting:** Charged separately or passed through at cost plus a management fee

## Strategic positioning

Given your experience building digital platforms for Ghana's mining sector, I would recommend pitching this not merely as a **B2B marketplace**, but as a **Digital Gold Trade & Member Services Platform**. Trading becomes one module within a broader ecosystem that helps the Chamber manage its members, digitize business processes, improve transparency, and create new revenue streams. This positioning is likely to be more attractive to the Chamber because it delivers value even when trading volumes fluctuate, while creating a long-term partnership for your company.

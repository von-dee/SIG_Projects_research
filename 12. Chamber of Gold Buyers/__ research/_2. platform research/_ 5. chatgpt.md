
Yes. In fact, this is often the easiest model to get an industry association to adopt because **there is no upfront investment by the Chamber**. You assume the technology risk, and both parties share in the success of the platform.

The model is commonly called **Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) with Revenue Sharing** or a **Marketplace Revenue Share Model**.

## Recommended Structure

### The Chamber provides:

* Brand and credibility
* Access to members
* Marketing and promotion
* Governance and trading rules
* Member verification

### Your company provides:

* Platform development
* Hosting and infrastructure
* Security
* Customer support
* Feature enhancements
* Operations and maintenance

### Revenue comes from platform usage

Rather than charging the Chamber, revenue is generated from users of the platform.

| Revenue Source                    | Charged To             |
| --------------------------------- | ---------------------- |
| Buyer transaction fee             | Buyer                  |
| Seller transaction fee            | Seller                 |
| Platform service fee              | Both parties           |
| Premium membership                | Businesses             |
| Featured supplier listings        | Sellers                |
| RFQ submission fee (optional)     | Buyers                 |
| Digital contract fee              | Transaction parties    |
| Escrow fee                        | Transaction parties    |
| Logistics coordination fee        | Users who opt in       |
| Trade finance referral commission | Financial institutions |
| Insurance referral commission     | Insurers               |

## Example transaction

A buyer purchases **10 kg of gold** valued at **US$1,000,000**.

Platform charges:

* Buyer fee: **0.10% = US$1,000**
* Seller fee: **0.10% = US$1,000**

Total platform revenue: **US$2,000**.

If your agreement with the Chamber is a 70/30 split:

* IT Company: **US$1,400**
* Chamber: **US$600**

This aligns incentives: the Chamber promotes platform adoption because it earns more as activity increases.

## Premium subscriptions

You can also offer optional plans.

| Plan         | Monthly Fee | Features                                     |
| ------------ | ----------: | -------------------------------------------- |
| Free         |        US$0 | Basic access                                 |
| Professional |      US$100 | More RFQs, analytics, priority support       |
| Enterprise   |     US$500+ | API access, integrations, advanced reporting |

This creates recurring revenue independent of transaction volume.

## Additional monetization

As adoption grows, the platform can earn from:

* Advertising by logistics providers and assay laboratories.
* Referrals to banks offering trade finance or escrow.
* API subscriptions for refineries and enterprise buyers.
* Data and market intelligence subscriptions.
* Digital certificates and document verification.

## Revenue-sharing agreement

A simple framework could be:

| Responsibility      | Chamber | IT Company |
| ------------------- | ------- | ---------- |
| Member acquisition  | ✓      |            |
| Industry governance | ✓      |            |
| Technology          |         | ✓         |
| Hosting             |         | ✓         |
| Support             |         | ✓         |
| Marketing           | ✓      | ✓         |
| Revenue share       | 30%     | 70%        |

The exact split depends on who bears the costs and operational responsibilities.

## Why this model is attractive

For the Chamber:

* No capital expenditure.
* No software maintenance burden.
* New, recurring income stream.
* Better services for members.

For your company:

* Long-term recurring revenue.
* Growing income as platform usage increases.
* Opportunity to expand into additional digital services.

### One important consideration

Because this platform deals with **high-value gold transactions**, the commission structure should be designed carefully. Very high percentage commissions can discourage adoption. Many commodity exchanges instead charge a **small fixed platform fee per transaction**, a **small fee per kilogram traded**, or a **modest percentage capped at a maximum amount**. This keeps fees predictable while still generating sustainable revenue.

For a Chamber-led platform, a **hybrid model** often works best:

* Free access for verified members.
* Low transaction/platform fees.
* Optional premium subscriptions and value-added services.
* Revenue sharing between the Chamber and your company.

This approach minimizes barriers to joining the platform while creating multiple income streams that grow with member adoption.

# **Technical Brief: Persistent Volumetric Overwatch for Mining (PVOM)**

**Subtitle:** From Battlefield Surveillance to the Autonomous Mine of 2026

## **1. Executive Summary**

Traditional mining surveillance relies on periodic drone flights or fixed CCTV, creating data gaps. **PVOM (Persistent Volumetric Overwatch for Mining)** applies WAMI technology to create a "Live Digital Twin" of the entire mining concession. By fusing hyperspectral sensors, 3D Gaussian Splatting, and AI tracking, mining operators can "rewind" time to analyze equipment failures, automate volumetric ore calculations, and secure vast exploration perimeters in real-time.

---

## **2. Revolutionizing Mineral Exploration**

### **A. Hyperspectral WAMI: The "Chemical Eye"**

While military WAMI focuses on visual movement, **Exploration WAMI** uses hyperspectral sensors. Instead of a geologist walking a grid, a high-altitude platform (HAP) scans hundreds of square kilometers simultaneously.

* **Mineral Mapping:** Identifies surface alteration zones and mineral signatures (e.g., copper porphyry or lithium-bearing pegmatites) across an entire province in one pass.
* **Backtracking Geological Anomalies:** By using the "TiVo" capability, explorers can track seasonal changes in soil moisture or vegetation stress that might reveal sub-surface structures or hidden aquifers.

### **B. SAR Integration (Seeing Through Surface)**

Recent advances in **Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR)** fused with WAMI allow explorers to see through dense jungle canopy or topsoil to map basement rock topography in 3D, providing a "transparent earth" view that identifies potential ore bodies.

## **3. Optimizing Mineral Production & Logistics**

### **A. "Pattern of Life" for Autonomous Haulage**

Using the same AI used to track insurgent networks, mining systems can monitor the **Pattern of Life** of a haulage fleet.

* **Predictive Bottlenecking:** AI identifies "micro-delays" in truck cycles before they cause a production dip.
* **Automated Dispatch:** Instead of manual dispatch, the system sees the "whole city" (the mine) and dynamically reroutes autonomous trucks based on real-time traffic, road conditions, and shovel availability.

### **B. Live Volumetric 3D Reconstruction**

Utilizing **3D Gaussian Splatting** (as seen in recent 2024-2026 research), the WAMI system creates a navigable 3D model of the pit that updates every second.

* **Real-time Inventory:** Automatically calculates the volume of stockpiles and the amount of material moved from the face without needing a surveyor to enter the pit.
* **Dynamic Pit Design:** Engineers can compare the "As-Built" 3D model against the "Short-Term Plan" in real-time, identifying deviations in bench height or ramp grade immediately.

## **4. Safety, Security, and Incident Reconstruction**

### **A. The "Mine Site Tape": Incident Forensics**

Just as the "Baghdad Tape" allowed commanders to see an IED's origin, **Mining WAMI** allows safety officers to "rewind" a site accident.

* **Root Cause Analysis:** If a collision occurs, investigators can rewind 48 hours to see if the operator’s "Pattern of Life" showed signs of fatigue or if a mechanical failure was preceded by unusual vehicle movements.
* **Virtual Tripwires:** AI creates geofences around high-risk zones (e.g., blast areas or high-wall edges). The system automatically alerts personnel if a human or vehicle enters a "red zone."

### **B. Tailing Dam Stability (The "Millimeter Overwatch")**

Persistent WAMI sensors can detect minute shifts in the structural integrity of tailing dams. By analyzing 3D movements at the millimeter level over months, AI can predict a dam failure weeks before it becomes visible to the human eye, preventing catastrophic environmental disasters.

## **5. Technological Infrastructure: The 2026 Stack**

To implement this, the "Modern Mine" utilizes the following research-driven stack:

1. **HAPS (High Altitude Pseudo-Satellites):** Solar-powered gliders that stay at 65,000 ft for months, providing persistent coverage without the cost of fuel-heavy Cessnas.
2. **Edge AI Processing:** Heavy data (terabytes per hour) is processed *on the aircraft* using neuromorphic chips, sending only "insights" (e.g., "Stockpile 4 is full," "Truck 202 is drifting") to the ground station.
3. **Mesh Drone Swarms:** For deep pits where satellite/HAPS views are blocked, swarms of small drones act as "sub-nodes," fusing their imagery back into the Wide Area mosaic.

## **6. Ethical and Environmental Considerations**

* **Environmental Monitoring:** WAMI provides an indisputable record of land reclamation progress, ensuring mining companies meet ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) commitments by tracking revegetation over years.
* **Security vs. Privacy:** While tracking workers' movements increases safety, it raises "surveillance state" concerns in labor relations. Advanced systems now use **Privacy-by-Design**, where human figures are represented as "anonymized tokens" unless a safety incident triggers a key-access identity reveal.

## **7. Conclusion**

The transition from **Targeted Surveillance** to **Persistent Volumetric Overwatch** represents the "Third Wave" of mining technology. By adopting WAMI, the mining industry moves from reactive management to a state of total operational transparency. In this environment, the mine is no longer a collection of isolated machines, but a **living, searchable 3D archive of activity**, optimized for maximum yield and zero harm.

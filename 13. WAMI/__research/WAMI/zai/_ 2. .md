
Based on the video transcript and the subsequent deep research reports (both general and mining-specific), here is a comprehensive list of all the technologies, systems, and technical concepts stated so far, categorized by their function:

### 1. Core Surveillance & WAMI Technologies

* **Wide Area Motion Imagery (WAMI / "Whammy"):** A system using multiple cameras to provide persistent, city-wide (or mine-wide) surveillance capable of tracking every moving object simultaneously and rewinding time.
* **Full Motion Video (FMV):** Traditional, high-resolution drone camera feeds with a narrow field of view (the "soda straw" problem).
* **Argus-IS (Autonomous Real-Time Ground Ubiquitous Surveillance Imaging System):** DARPA's 1.8-gigapixel WAMI system utilizing 368 cellular phone-style cameras fused together.
* **Constant Hawk:** The world's first operational WAMI system, deployed by the US Army in 2006.
* **Angel Fire:** A WAMI system deployed by the US Air Force and Marine Corps in 2007.
* **Gorgon Stare:** An advanced WAMI system mounted on MQ-9 Reaper drones, capable of watching 100 square kilometers simultaneously.
* **Wide Area Airborne Surveillance (WAAS):** The broader military terminology for persistent airborne surveillance systems.
* **Transparent Sky (3rd Gen WAMI):** A system introduced in 2023 capable of full 3D scene reconstruction from persistent aerial surveillance.

### 2. Platforms & Hardware (Air, Space, Ground)

* **MQ-9 Reaper Drone:** The iconic military UAV used to mount the Gorgon Stare system.
* **Turboprop Aircraft:** The original platform used to deploy Constant Hawk over Iraq and Afghanistan.
* **Cessna Aircraft:** The light, innocuous-looking planes used by Persistent Surveillance Systems (PSS) and the FBI to fly WAMI over US cities.
* **Aerostats (Tethered Blimps):** Persistent platforms used in mining to hover 10,000 feet above a site holding WAMI sensors.
* **Satellite Constellations:** Space-based platforms used for persistent overwatch (replacing traditional airborne WAMI, as seen in Ukraine).
* **Stingray Devices (IMSI Catchers / Cell-Site Simulators):** Devices manufactured by L3Harris that mimic cell towers to mass-track thousands of cell phones simultaneously, even indoors.
* **Automated License Plate Readers (ALPRs) / Flock Cameras:** Ground-based stationary cameras used to capture vehicle license plates.
* **CCTV / Street Cameras:** Fixed, ground-level video cameras (e.g., Baltimore's CitiWatch system, mining site CCTV).
* **RFID Badges / Geofencing:** Ground-based worker tracking systems used in mining to monitor personnel locations.
* **Automated Haul Trucks:** Autonomous mining vehicles integrated into centralized operational networks.

### 3. AI, Data Analytics & Spatial Computing

* **Machine Learning / Artificial Intelligence (AI):** Algorithms used to automate the tracking of hundreds of targets, identify targets across video streams, and identify surveillance aircraft.
* **Patterns of Life (PoL):** AI-generated movement histories that learn routines, meeting places, and deviations of people or vehicles.
* **Digital Twin:** A real-time, volumetric database that mirrors all physical movement and infrastructure of a city or mine site.
* **3D Gaussian Splatting:** A recent computer graphics breakthrough allowing for real-time rendering of complex, navigable 3D scenes from sparse 2D image data (used for 3D WAMI).
* **Photogrammetry:** The science of making reliable measurements by the use of photographs and other 2D data to create 3D models.
* **Visual Positioning Systems (VPS):** Technology that maps environments spatially (similar to Google's spatial computing).
* **Spatial Computing:** The broader technological field of interacting with 3D digital models of physical spaces.
* **Virtual Tripwires / Watch Zones:** AI thresholds that only alert human operators when a tracked object crosses a specific digital boundary.
* **Augmented Reality (AR) Overlays:** Technology that superimposes data (like street names, property owners, or vehicle IDs) directly onto live surveillance footage.

### 4. OSINT (Open-Source Intelligence) & Tracking Tools

* **Flightradar24 & ADS-B Exchange:** Public, free-to-use flight tracking applications and data sets used by journalists and citizens to track aircraft transponders and expose "shadow" surveillance fleets.
* **OSINT (Open-Source Intelligence):** The practice of using publicly available data (like flight tracks or satellite imagery) to uncover secret operations.

### 5. Military & Mining Specific Software Systems

* **Delta System:** Ukraine's battle management software that fuses data from drones, satellites, and sensors into a real-time operational picture.
* **Avengers:** A subplatform used by Ukraine that uses AI to automatically identify targets across thousands of simultaneous video streams.
* **Saker Scout:** An AI platform with onboard machine learning used to autonomously identify and classify military hardware.
* **TRIFFID:** A 2026 EU-funded project designed to pipeline live UAV video into 3D Gaussian splat representations for first responders.
* **Fleet Management Telemetry:** Mining software used to track and optimize haul truck cycles and integrate with WAMI data.

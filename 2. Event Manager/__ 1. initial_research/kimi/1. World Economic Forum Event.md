

# Forum Live — Official World Economic Forum Event App

## Overview

**Forum Live** is the official mobile app for World Economic Forum (WEF) events, built as a modern, high-end event companion designed for the world's most influential decision-makers. It is not a consumer or freemium app — it is a **gated, invitation-only utility** provided exclusively to registered WEF event participants (and, in a limited capacity, to their support staff). The app is part of the broader **TopLink** digital ecosystem, which includes a year-round web platform, the mobile event app, and on-site event kiosks.

## Site Map & App Architecture

The app is organized around five core functional modules, each designed to maximize the efficiency and impact of high-stakes event participation:

### 1. **EVENTS**
- Search and explore events matching your interests
- View registered events organized for quick access
- Access the full event programme and document library

### 2. **SESSIONS**
- Bookmark sessions in a built-in calendar
- View all sessions, meetings, and private invitations in one place
- Receive real-time updates and push notifications for schedule changes
- Manage private event invitations (accept/decline)

### 3. **NETWORK**
- Browse the participant directory with intelligent filtering (by industry, expertise, country, etc.)
- Exchange **digital business cards** by scanning event badges
- Save business card details directly to device contacts
- Send direct messages to other participants
- Send and manage meeting requests

### 4. **PLAN**
- Schedule bilateral meetings directly within the app
- Sync personal device calendar with the app agenda
- Manage personal agenda for private events and bilateral meetings

### 5. **NAVIGATE**
- **Turn-by-turn indoor navigation** within complex event venues
- Locate points of interest and nearby activities
- View interactive maps of meeting venues and event hotels

### 6. **SETTINGS / MORE**
- Access past events you've participated in
- Update privacy settings (control which contact details are visible to others)
- Sync agenda with device calendar
- Configure push notifications
- Support staff passcode login (limited access to core event info only)
- Contact Forum concierge and support (toplinksupport@weforum.org)

## Access & Authentication

- **Primary Users:** Registered event participants only. A confirmed registration at a WEF meeting is required to access the app. Login uses the same credentials as the TopLink web platform.
- **Support Staff:** Can access core event information via a passcode login (available one week before the meeting), but personal features like messaging and agenda management are disabled.
- **Spouses:** Registered spouses can be set up with a separate account via the app's settings section.
- **Past Events:** Users can switch between current and past events they've attended through the settings menu.

## Business Model: How Money Is Made

The Forum Live app itself is **not a revenue-generating product** in the traditional sense. It is a **value-add service** bundled into the World Economic Forum's broader membership and partnership ecosystem. The WEF is a Swiss not-for-profit foundation, and its revenue model is built on **institutional access, not consumer sales**. Here's how the money flows:

### 1. **Membership & Partnership Fees (Primary Revenue Driver)**
This is the WEF's core business model. The organization is funded by approximately **1,000 member companies**, typically global enterprises with more than $5 billion in turnover. Membership is highly stratified:

| Tier | Annual Fee (Approximate) | Access Level |
|------|--------------------------|--------------|
| **Basic/Individual** | ~$350–$52,000 | Limited web access, no Davos |
| **Industry Associate** | ~$137,000 | Enhanced access |
| **Industry Partner** | ~$250,000–$263,000 | Standard corporate participation |
| **Strategic Partner** | ~$500,000–$758,000 | Highest tier, premium access, advisory engagement |

- In the fiscal year 2023–2024, **partnership and membership revenue reached CHF 271 million** (~$300M USD), representing a 2.6% year-on-year increase. 
- In 2024–2025, this grew to **CHF 287 million**, a 5.7% increase. 

### 2. **Event Participation & Badge Fees**
Beyond membership, companies pay per-delegate fees to attend events like the Annual Meeting in Davos:

- **Per-delegate badge fee:** Historically ~$19,000–$20,000 per person; as of 2025, elite badges reportedly cost up to **$35,000** each.
- **Estimated total cost per delegate:** ~$40,000 when including travel, accommodation, and logistics (with luxury options pushing this far higher).
- **Major sponsorships:** Companies like Microsoft and McKinsey have been reported to spend up to **$1 million** for venue sponsorships and premium access packages. 

### 3. **Project & Initiative Funding**
The WEF hosts over **40 grant-funded multistakeholder platforms** where corporations, governments, and foundations co-fund specific initiatives:

- Since 2015, over **CHF 225 million** in grant funding has been raised for platforms addressing climate, nature, health, and trade.
- These projects are financed by public institutions, private foundations, and corporate partners who pay to participate in and shape the agenda. 

### 4. **Grants & Public Sector Funding**
- Grants (predominantly from public institutions and foundations) accounted for approximately **11% of total revenue** in 2023–2024.
- Direct funding decreased slightly by CHF 2.153 million over that period but remains significant. 

### 5. **Other Revenue Streams**
- **Publication sales and platform subscriptions** (e.g., Strategic Intelligence platform with 1M+ users)
- **Consultancy services** and bespoke advisory work
- **In-kind contributions** (logistics, venues, personnel secondments)
- **Event sponsorships** and bespoke event services

### WEF Financial Snapshot (2023–2024)
| Metric | Amount |
|--------|--------|
| **Total Revenue** | CHF 440 million (~$485M USD) |
| **Partnership & Membership Revenue** | CHF 271 million |
| **Grants & Direct Funding** | ~11% of total revenue |
| **Surplus** | None reported (break-even) |

## How Forum Live Fits Into the Business Model

Forum Live is a **strategic enabler** of the WEF's revenue model, not a standalone profit center:

1. **Retention Tool:** The app increases the perceived value of WEF membership and event participation, justifying the high fees by making the experience seamless, efficient, and exclusive.
2. **Network Effect Amplifier:** By facilitating connections, meetings, and digital business card exchanges, the app makes the WEF ecosystem more valuable — encouraging members to renew and upgrade their partnerships.
3. **Data & Insights:** The app generates rich behavioral data on how participants engage with content, sessions, and each other. This informs the WEF's programming and demonstrates ROI to partners.
4. **Exclusivity Signal:** The invitation-only, badge-gated nature of the app reinforces the WEF's brand as an elite, inaccessible club — which is precisely what members pay a premium for.
5. **Operational Efficiency:** Digital wayfinding, real-time updates, and automated scheduling reduce the logistical overhead of running massive events like Davos.

## Summary

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What it is** | Official, invitation-only event app for WEF participants |
| **Who pays** | No direct consumer cost — bundled with WEF membership/event fees |
| **WEF's main revenue** | Corporate membership fees (CHF 271–287M/year), event badge fees, project grants |
| **App's role** | Value-add retention tool that justifies high membership costs and enhances the exclusive WEF experience |

The Forum Live app exemplifies a **B2B ecosystem play**: the product is free to the end-user (the executive), but the entire experience is funded by the corporations who pay six- and seven-figure sums for the privilege of access, influence, and networking at the world's most exclusive economic gathering.

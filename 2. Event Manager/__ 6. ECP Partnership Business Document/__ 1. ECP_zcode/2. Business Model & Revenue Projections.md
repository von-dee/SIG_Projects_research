## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY OF THE MODEL

The platform monetises through **three layered revenue engines**, each with different payers, margins, and scalability. Crucially, the model is designed so that **ECP participates in the upside**, not just [Company Name]:

| Engine | Payer | Margin profile | Scalability |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Platform licence & services** | ECP (the organiser) | Moderate margin; covers delivery cost | Linear with event count |
| **2. Sponsor & exhibitor monetisation** | Sponsors / exhibitors | High margin; mostly digital inventory | Scales with delegate count × sponsor count |
| **3. Data & insights products** | Sponsors, investors, analysts | Very high margin; proprietary data asset | Compounds across the portfolio over time |

**The structural insight:** Engine 1 alone is a vendor relationship. Engines 2 and 3 — which scale with ECP's audience, not with our build cost — are where the partnership becomes a joint business. The configurable platform means Engine 1 cost falls per event over time, while Engines 2 and 3 keep growing.

## 2. REVENUE STREAM 1 — PLATFORM LICENCE & PROFESSIONAL SERVICES

This is the foundational revenue: what ECP (or, in the JV model, the joint entity) pays to build, configure, host, and support the platform.

### 2.1 Pricing anchors (illustrative, per event)

| Line item | Illustrative range | Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Platform build (one-time, MVP scope) | $120,000–$280,000 | Mirrors FMF26 SaaS licensing precedent of $30k–$100k+; uplift for bespoke 8-module, AI-rich scope and energy-sector configuration |
| Per-event configuration (event 2 onwards) | $15,000–$35,000 | Configuration of branding, modules, roles, sponsor tiers — not a rebuild |
| Annual platform licence / hosting | $24,000–$60,000 / year | Backend hosting, AI/LLM usage, translation pipeline, maintenance |
| BLE beacon hardware (per venue) | $8,000–$20,000 | Hardware + install; reusable across years at same venue |
| On-site support team (per event) | $10,000–$25,000 | Engineers + support staff for event days |
| Professional services / custom integrations | $15,000–$40,000 | CRM, registration system, payment, single sign-on |
| Post-event support retainer (optional) | $12,000–$30,000 / year | 6–12 month maintenance + post-event networking window |

### 2.2 Engagement models (recap)

| Model | Year-1 cost to ECP | When it fits |
|---|---|---|
| **Per-Event Partnership** | $120k–$280k per event | Low-commitment on-ramp; pilot one flagship |
| **Portfolio Partnership (Recommended)** | $120k–$280k build (once) + $15k–$35k config per event | All ECP events in a cycle; lowest marginal cost |
| **Joint Venture / Co-Development** | Co-invested; revenue split up to 50/50 | Strategic play; platform as a line of business |

### 2.3 Why the portfolio model wins on cost

The unit economics flip in the portfolio model because **configuration is cheap, rebuilds are not**:

| Scenario | 5 events, per-event rebuild | 5 events, portfolio platform |
|---|---|---|
| Build cost | 5 × $120k = **$600k** | 1 × $200k = **$200k** |
| Config cost | included above | 4 × $25k = **$100k** |
| **Total Year-1 delivery** | **$600k** | **$300k** |
| **Savings** | — | **~$300k (50%)** |

This ~50% delivery saving is the economic foundation of the partnership pitch.

## 3. REVENUE STREAM 2 — SPONSOR & EXHIBITOR MONETISATION

This is the **primary revenue engine** and the one where ECP's existing sponsor base creates immediate upside. The FMF26 research identifies five monetisation mechanisms; we add two more specific to the deal-making focus.

### 3.1 The seven monetisation mechanisms

| # | Mechanism | Description | Illustrative price | Who pays |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **App takeovers / splash sponsorship** | Sponsor logo on app open | $8,000–$25,000 / event | Platinum sponsor |
| 2 | **Featured listings & banners** | Premium placement on Home / Expo tabs, rotating banners | $2,000–$8,000 / placement / event | Sponsors, exhibitors |
| 3 | **Sponsored push notifications** | Branded, targeted announcements | $1,500–$5,000 / send | Sponsors |
| 4 | **Lead-retrieval licences** | QR badge scan + CRM export per exhibitor | $200–$500 / exhibitor | Exhibitors |
| 5 | **Premium matchmaking & deal-room slots** | Paid access to high-demand VIP meeting slots; bookable deal rooms | $1,000–$5,000 / slot or room | Sponsors, exhibitors |
| 6 | **Sponsor analytics dashboards** | Post-event ROI report (leads, views, meetings) | $3,000–$10,000 / sponsor / event | Sponsors |
| 7 | **Sponsored content & resource hubs** | Gated whitepapers, videos, hosted sessions | $2,000–$8,000 / asset | Sponsors |

### 3.2 Per-event sponsor revenue potential (illustrative)

Assume a mid-sized ECP event with **~4,000 delegates, ~120 exhibitors, ~30 sponsors**:

| Sponsored push notifications | 6 sends × $3,000 | $18,000 |

> **Note:** This is gross sponsor revenue enabled by the platform. Under the Portfolio Partnership, ECP retains 75–85% and [Company Name] earns 15–25% rev-share. Under JV, splits go up to 50/50.

### 3.3 Revenue share to ECP (per event, illustrative)

## 4. REVENUE STREAM 3 — DATA & INSIGHTS PRODUCTS

The platform generates proprietary first-party data that becomes a saleable asset in years 2–3. This is the highest-margin stream and the one that compounds.

### 4.1 Data assets created

| Asset | Source | Value to buyer |
|---|---|---|
| **Attendee intent & interest signals** | Profile tags, search queries, session bookmarks | Hyper-targeted sponsorship sales for next cycle |
| **Sponsor ROI proof** | Leads, scans, meetings, profile views per sponsor | Justifies sponsorship price increases & multi-year renewals |
| **Deal-flow intelligence** | Matchmaking activity, meeting outcomes (anonymised) | Investor & analyst market intelligence |
| **Session & topic demand** | Attendance, Q&A, ratings | Programme planning; thought-leadership content |
| **Cross-event delegate graph** | Persistent identity across the ECP portfolio | Unique, non-replicable network dataset |

### 4.2 Data product pricing (illustrative, Year 2–3)

| Product | Buyer | Illustrative price |
|---|---|---|
| Sponsor ROI report (premium tier) | Each sponsor | Included in Stream 2, or $10k–$25k standalone |
| Portfolio intelligence report | Investors, analysts, consultancies | $15,000–$40,000 / report |
| Targeted sponsorship enablement pack | Next-cycle sponsor sales | Bundled into sponsorship price uplift |
| anonymised deal-flow dataset (annual licence) | Market researchers | $25,000–$60,000 / year |

### 4.3 Why data compounds

Each event adds to the dataset; each subsequent event's intelligence is richer. A per-event vendor creates no such asset. This is the **moat** — and the reason the partnership is strategic, not transactional.

## 8. COST STRUCTURE (WHAT DRIVES OUR DELIVERY COST)

| Cost driver | Nature | Lever to reduce over time |
|---|---|---|
| Engineering (mobile, backend, AI) | Mostly fixed per build | Amortised across portfolio (config, not rebuild) |
| Third-party services (LLM, translation, maps) | Variable, per-usage | Negotiated volume tiers as portfolio scales |
| On-site delivery (hardware, staff) | Variable, per-event | Reusable beacons; templated support runbook |
| Compliance & security | Fixed annual | One certification covers portfolio |

**Margin trajectory:** Gross margin improves from ~35% in Year 1 (heavy build) to ~60–70% in Year 3 (configuration-led), because the configurable architecture means each new event costs less to deliver than the last.

The Core Problem

Built for content consumption,
not capital formation.

Energy Capital & Power runs high-stakes events where attendees include heads of state, ministers, NOCs/IOCs, and institutional investors. The core purpose is deal-making, not just content delivery.

However, most benchmark conference apps (WEF, SXSW, CES) are built for single events and focus on content consumption. This creates critical pain points for ECP's unique, multi-event, high-stakes audience.

"

Most benchmark apps are built for content consumption at a single event. ECP needs a system built for capital formation across a recurring portfolio.

The Single-Event Trap

Generic apps treat each event in isolation. ECP needs one system spanning a 7-event portfolio, requiring cross-series identity and continuity.

VIP & Protocol Complexity

Heads of state and ministers require role-based access tiers, security-aware routing, and embassy-level discretion that standard apps lack.

Lost Deal Continuity

Deals take time. Standard apps cut access immediately post-event. ECP needs a 6-9 month window to sustain networking and track outcomes.

Multilingual Barriers

Francophone, Lusophone, Arabic, and English delegations struggle without live translation and real-time captions tailored to the region.

Shallow Matchmaking

Basic directories don't filter by business intent, deal stage, or project size. ECP needs AI-driven matchmaking focused on capital seeking/offering.

Untracked Outcomes

Without linking meetings to follow-up status, ECP cannot measure its core KPI: "deals facilitated." The ROI gap remains unsolved.

The Brief

Adapted from gold-standard conference apps — re-engineered for capital formation.

Energy Capital & Power runs a portfolio of high-profile energy and mining investment events. Attendees include heads of state, ministers, NOCs/IOCs, and institutional investors — and the core purpose of every event is deal-making, not just content delivery.

The feature list below adapts patterns from WEF, CES, SXSW, MWC, and FMF — plus gold-standard apps like Salesforce Events, Money 20/20, and AWS re:Invent — to ECP's specific context: a multi-event, high-stakes, government-and-investor audience where facilitating bankable deals is the primary KPI.

Suggested Prioritization

A three-phase build —
from MVP to platform.

Phase 01 · MVP

Foundation

Ship the infrastructure required to run any ECP event end-to-end.

  • Access & ID — SSO, e-badge, QR, role tiers
  • Scheduling — agenda builder, reservations
  • Basic networking — directory + messaging
  • Navigation — venue maps & routing
Phase 02 · Intelligence

Deal-making layer

Add the AI and content features that turn a conference app into a deal-making instrument.

  • AI matchmaking by business intent
  • Deal-room booking for sponsors
  • Multilingual content & live translation
  • Voice + text AI chatbot
Phase 03 · Continuity

Platform moat

Close the loop between events — the differentiation no benchmark app provides.

  • Cross-event CRM for ECP teams
  • Deal/outcome tracking
  • Prospect news integration
  • Hyperlocal real-time personalization

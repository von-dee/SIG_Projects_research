Price agents poll LME REST API and CME DataMine every 15 minutes. A normalization agent maps tickers across exchanges and stores in TimescaleDB. Anomaly detection triggers alerts when spot diverges from 30-day moving average by >2σ.

Time-series DB

Vector store

TimescaleDB (PostgreSQL extension) stores all time-series data: spot prices, futures curves, production volumes, cost indices. Enables fast range queries like "copper AISC trend for Chilean mines last 3 years".

TimescaleDBOpen source

PostgreSQL asset registry stores every tracked mine: coordinates, owner, operator, commodity mix, nameplate capacity, current operational status, permit history. This is your equivalent of WoodMac's asset database — built from public JORC/NI 43-101 reports and company filings.

PostgreSQLPostGIS for geo queries

pgvector (PostgreSQL extension) enables semantic search over the full document corpus — filings, reports, news. Users can ask "what have companies said about Chilean water scarcity risks?" and get relevant passages across thousands of documents.

pgvectorOpenAI embeddings

Graph relationships power questions like "which lithium projects does Albemarle have exposure to, and what countries are they in?" — traversing company → equity stake → mine → commodity → country nodes. Can be implemented in PostgreSQL with foreign keys or Neo4j for complex traversals.

Report generator

Retrieval-augmented generation is your core product differentiator. User asks a question → system retrieves relevant data from TimescaleDB (prices), pgvector (documents), and the asset registry → Claude synthesizes a cited answer. Replaces reading 40-page PDF reports.

Example: "What's the risk to cobalt supply from DRC political instability this year?" → retrieves recent news, company exposure data, historical precedents → answer with source citations.

Claude APILangChain / custom

Forecast models combine statistical time-series models (ARIMA, Prophet) for price prediction with fundamental supply/demand balance sheets built from asset-level production data. Claude agents can stress-test scenarios: "What if Chilean copper output drops 15% due to water restrictions?"

Prophet / statsforecastClaude scenario agent

Cost curve automation: agent reads every mine's latest filing, extracts C1/AISC guidance, normalizes to USD/tonne, ranks all mines on a cost curve. This is WoodMac's core product. You can replicate ~80% of it from public data. Update quarterly when filings drop.

Users configure their own alert rules in plain English: "Notify me if lithium carbonate drops below $12,000/t" or "Alert on any new Chilean copper mine permit."

Conversational intelligence is your primary UX moat. Every user gets a persistent chat interface over the full data platform. Queries are routed to the right data source automatically. Answers are cited. Follow-up questions maintain context. This replaces navigating menus to find the right report.

Key differentiator

Dashboard surface the most important views: commodity price tracker, global cost curves (interactive, filterable by region/owner), supply/demand balance sheet, and an asset map. Built in React with Recharts or D3. Embeds live data from your TimescaleDB.

React + RechartsMapbox GL

Internal data integration is the enterprise moat. Mining companies and funds can upload their own mine models or portfolio spreadsheets. The platform blends their private data with your public-data layer for benchmarking: "How does my mine's AISC compare to the global cost curve?" — a question WoodMac cannot answer with internal data.

Enterprise differentiator

Programmatic API lets quantitative analysts and trading desks pull clean, structured data directly into their models. REST + websocket for real-time prices. This is WoodMac's Lens Direct, but accessible at a fraction of the cost and without a 6-month onboarding process.

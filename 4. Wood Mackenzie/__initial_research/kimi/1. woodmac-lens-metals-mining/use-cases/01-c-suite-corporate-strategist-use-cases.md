## Use Case 1: Portfolio Competitive Positioning Analysis

**Persona**: C-Suite & Corporate Strategist
**Objective**: Understand where the company's assets rank on global cost curves to inform strategic positioning

### Scenario
The CSO of a diversified mining company needs to present to the board on the company's competitive position across its copper, gold, and nickel assets. The board wants to know which assets are world-class, which are at risk, and where capital should be allocated.

### Workflow

1. **Access Assets Discovery** solution
2. Filter global mines by commodity (copper, gold, nickel)
3. Generate global cost curves for each commodity
4. Overlay the company's assets on the cost curves
5. Benchmark against top 10 global producers in each commodity
6. Export visualisations for board presentation

### Lens Metals & Mining Capabilities Used

- **Assets Discovery**: Cost curve generation and peer benchmarking
- **Data Coverage**: C1, C2, C3 cost data; AISC for gold assets
- **Export Tools**: Cost curve charts for PowerPoint integration

### Outcome

The CSO identifies that:
- Copper assets sit in the second quartile globally - room for improvement
- Gold assets are first quartile - maintain and expand
- Nickel assets are third quartile - operational improvement program needed
- Board approves capital allocation prioritising gold expansion and copper optimisation

### Value Delivered

- Data-driven capital allocation decision
- Clear visual evidence for board communication
- Identified $50M potential cost reduction opportunity in nickel division

## Use Case 2: Energy Transition Scenario Planning

**Persona**: C-Suite & Corporate Strategist
**Objective**: Assess how different energy transition pathways affect the company's commodity portfolio value

### Scenario
The CEO requests analysis of how accelerated decarbonisation scenarios (e.g., net-zero by 2050) versus base case scenarios would affect the company's asset valuations and strategic direction.

### Workflow

1. **Access Markets Scenarios** solution
2. Select base case and alternative energy transition scenarios
3. Overlay demand forecasts for each commodity in the portfolio
4. Run valuation impact analysis under each scenario
5. Identify winners and losers across the portfolio
6. Develop strategic response options

### Lens Metals & Mining Capabilities Used

- **Markets Scenarios**: Compare base case vs. energy transition demand pathways
- **Assets Valuations**: Run NPV calculations under different price scenarios
- **Markets Forecast**: Long-term supply-demand balances to 2050

### Outcome

Analysis reveals:
- Copper and nickel assets significantly appreciate under net-zero scenario (EV/grid demand)
- Thermal coal assets face accelerated decline
- Company pivots M&A strategy toward copper and battery metals
- Board approves divestment timeline for thermal coal assets

### Value Delivered

- Avoided stranded asset risk through early divestment planning
- Identified $2B+ value uplift potential from pivot to energy transition metals
- Enhanced ESG profile attracting sustainability-focused investors

## Use Case 3: M&A Target Screening

**Persona**: C-Suite & Corporate Strategist
**Objective**: Identify and prioritise acquisition targets that fill strategic gaps in the portfolio

### Scenario
The company has $1B allocated for acquisitions and wants to identify copper assets in the Americas that sit in the first or second cost quartile with expansion potential.

### Workflow

1. **Access Assets Discovery** solution
2. Set filters: Commodity = Copper, Region = Americas, Cost Position = Q1-Q2
3. Screen for assets with resource/reserve upside
4. Cross-reference with project pipeline data
5. Rank targets by strategic fit, cost position, and growth potential
6. Export shortlist for business development team

### Lens Metals & Mining Capabilities Used

- **Assets Discovery**: Asset screening with multiple criteria
- **Data Coverage**: Reserve/resource data, production profiles, ownership structure
- **Assets Valuations**: Initial valuation of shortlisted targets

### Outcome

- Identified 12 qualified targets
- Prioritised 3 for detailed due diligence
- Business development team initiates approach to top target
- Acquisition completed within 18 months, adding 200ktpa copper production in Q1 cost position

### Value Delivered

- Systematic, data-driven target screening vs. ad-hoc deal flow
- Focused BD efforts on highest-strategic-value targets
- Acquisition added at 0.7x NAV - accretive to shareholders

## Use Case 4: Capital Allocation Framework Development

**Persona**: C-Suite & Corporate Strategist
**Objective**: Build a robust framework for allocating capital across competing projects in different commodities

### Scenario
The CFO needs a standardised approach to evaluating capital projects across copper, gold, and coal divisions that accounts for commodity outlook, cost position, and strategic fit.

### Workflow

1. **Access Markets Forecast** for all three commodities
2. Pull long-term price forecasts and supply-demand balances
3. Overlay each division's project pipeline on cost curves
4. Calculate incentive prices for each project
5. Rank projects by margin potential and strategic alignment
6. Build allocation model integrating all inputs

### Lens Metals & Mining Capabilities Used

- **Markets Forecast**: Price forecasts to 2050, supply-demand balances
- **Assets Discovery**: Cost curve positioning, project pipeline data
- **Assets Valuations**: Incentive price calculations, NPV modelling

### Outcome

- Capital allocation framework adopted by board
- 60% of capex allocated to copper projects (best risk-adjusted returns)
- 30% to gold (stable cash generation)
- 10% to coal (maintenance only, no growth)
- Framework becomes standard for annual budget process

### Value Delivered

- Transparent, defensible capital allocation process
- Improved returns on invested capital by 3 percentage points
- Reduced time for budget decision-making by 50%

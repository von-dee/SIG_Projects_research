## Use Case 1: Mining Fund Portfolio Construction

**Persona**: Fund Manager
**Objective**: Construct a diversified mining fund portfolio with optimal risk-return characteristics

### Scenario
A fund manager is launching a new global mining fund with $500M AUM and needs to build an initial portfolio across commodities and geographies.

### Workflow

1. **Access Assets Discovery** to screen the global mining universe
2. Apply filters: Market cap, commodity exposure, cost position, jurisdiction
3. **Access Markets Forecast** for commodity outlooks
4. **Access Markets Scenarios** to stress-test under different energy transition pathways
5. Rank opportunities by risk-adjusted return potential
6. Build portfolio with 20-25 positions

### Lens Metals & Mining Capabilities Used

- **Assets Discovery**: Universe screening and peer comparison
- **Markets Forecast**: Commodity allocation framework
- **Markets Scenarios**: Portfolio stress-testing
- **Assets Valuations**: Independent valuation checks

### Outcome

Portfolio construction:
- 40% Copper (3 positions: tier-1 producer, developer, royalty)
- 25% Gold (4 positions across cost curve)
- 15% Battery metals (lithium, nickel)
- 10% Diversified (BHP, Glencore)
- 10% Iron ore (2 positions)

Fund launch oversubscribed; closes at $600M

### Value Delivered

- Fund returns 25% in first year (vs. 15% for mining index)
- Outperformance attributed to commodity allocation and stock selection
- AUM grows to $1B within 18 months

## Use Case 2: Risk Management and Hedging Decision

**Persona**: Fund Manager
**Objective**: Decide whether to hedge commodity price exposure in the portfolio

### Scenario
The fund has significant copper exposure that has appreciated 30%. The manager is considering hedging some of this exposure but needs to understand the supply-demand outlook.

### Workflow

1. **Access Markets Forecast** for copper short-term outlook
2. Check inventory levels and market balance
3. Review project pipeline for near-term supply additions
4. Analyse Chinese demand indicators
5. Assess cost curve support levels
6. Make hedging decision

### Lens Metals & Mining Capabilities Used

- **Markets Forecast**: Short-term monthly outlook, price forecasts
- **Data Coverage**: Inventory data, project pipeline tracking
- **Assets Discovery**: Cost curve support level analysis

### Outcome

Analysis shows:
- Copper market remains in deficit through next 12 months
- Inventory at multi-year lows
- Cost curve support at $3.20/lb (vs. current $4.20/lb)
- Fund manager decides NOT to hedge; maintains full exposure

### Value Delivered

- Copper position appreciates additional 15% over next 6 months
- Avoided $15M in hedging costs and opportunity loss
- Decision validated by continued market tightness

## Use Case 3: ESG Integration and Portfolio Screening

**Persona**: Fund Manager
**Objective**: Integrate ESG factors into mining portfolio construction and screen for climate risks

### Scenario
An institutional client mandates that 50% of their allocation must meet ESG criteria, including TCFD alignment and no thermal coal exposure.

### Workflow

1. **Access Markets Scenarios** for energy transition impact analysis
2. Screen portfolio for thermal coal exposure
3. Analyse carbon intensity of each holding
4. Assess which companies are positioned for low-carbon transition
5. Rebalance portfolio to meet ESG criteria
6. Report to client with ESG metrics

### Lens Metals & Mining Capabilities Used

- **Markets Scenarios**: Energy transition demand impact by commodity
- **Supply Chain**: Value chain carbon intensity assessment
- **Markets Forecast**: Long-term demand outlook for transition metals

### Outcome

- Thermal coal positions divested ($30M)
- Proceeds reallocated to copper and battery metals
- Portfolio carbon intensity reduced by 40%
- ESG score improves from C to A

### Value Delivered

- Retain $100M institutional mandate
- Attract additional $50M from ESG-focused investors
- Portfolio outperforms as energy transition accelerates

## Use Case 4: Event-Driven Trading Opportunity

**Persona**: Fund Manager
**Objective**: Capitalise on a supply disruption event in a specific commodity

### Scenario
A major copper mine in Chile announces unexpected production cuts due to water restrictions. The fund manager needs to assess the market impact and trading opportunity.

### Workflow

1. **Access Markets Forecast** for current copper market balance
2. Calculate production loss as % of global supply
3. Check inventory buffer available to absorb shock
4. Review other producers' ability to increase output
5. **Access Assets Discovery** to see which miners benefit from higher prices
6. Execute trades within hours of announcement

### Lens Metals & Mining Capabilities Used

- **Markets Forecast**: Market balance, inventory data
- **Assets Discovery**: Peer asset data to identify beneficiaries
- **Data Coverage**: Production profiles, cost structures

### Outcome

- Chilean mine cuts represent 1.5% of global supply
- Market was already in deficit; inventory low
- Fund goes long copper futures and buys copper producers
- Position generates 12% return in 2 weeks as copper rallies 8%

### Value Delivered

- $6M profit on event-driven trade
- Demonstrated value of rapid access to comprehensive market data
- Process established for future supply disruption events

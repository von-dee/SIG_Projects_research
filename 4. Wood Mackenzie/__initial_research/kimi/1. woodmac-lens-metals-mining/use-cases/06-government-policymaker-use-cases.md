## Use Case 1: Critical Minerals Strategy Development

**Persona**: Government & Policymaker
**Objective**: Develop a national critical minerals strategy to secure supply chains for key industries

### Scenario
A government's Department of Industry is tasked with developing a Critical Minerals Strategy to support the domestic manufacturing, defence, and clean energy sectors. Need to identify which minerals are critical and recommend policy interventions.

### Workflow

1. **Access Supply Chain** to map global supply chains for 30 candidate minerals
2. Assess concentration risk (geographic, corporate)
3. **Access Markets Forecast** for demand projections to 2050
4. Analyse substitution potential and recycling rates
5. Score each mineral on economic importance and supply risk
6. Develop policy recommendations

### Lens Metals & Mining Capabilities Used

- **Supply Chain**: Material flow mapping, concentration analysis
- **Markets Forecast**: Long-term demand forecasts by end-use sector
- **Markets Scenarios**: Clean energy adoption demand scenarios
- **Assets Discovery**: Global asset database for supply source identification

### Outcome

Critical minerals list established:
- **Tier 1** (Highest priority): Rare earths, lithium, cobalt, graphite, nickel
- **Tier 2**: Copper, vanadium, tungsten
- **Tier 3**: Manganese, chromium

Policy recommendations:
- $500M Critical Minerals Fund for domestic projects
- Streamlined permitting for critical minerals mines
- International partnerships with allied countries

### Value Delivered

- Strategy adopted by Cabinet
- Attracted $2B in critical minerals investment within 2 years
- Reduced import dependency for 5 critical minerals by 30%

## Use Case 2: Mining Investment Competitiveness Assessment

**Persona**: Government & Policymaker
**Objective**: Benchmark the jurisdiction's mining competitiveness against peer countries and identify policy improvements

### Scenario
A mining department wants to understand why exploration investment has declined 20% over 3 years and what policy changes could reverse the trend.

### Workflow

1. **Access Assets Discovery** to benchmark the jurisdiction's geological potential
2. Compare fiscal terms (royalties, tax rates) against competitor jurisdictions
3. Analyse permitting timelines vs. global benchmarks
4. Review infrastructure quality and availability
5. Assess political risk vs. peer countries
6. Develop competitiveness improvement plan

### Lens Metals & Mining Capabilities Used

- **Assets Discovery**: Global asset and project pipeline data
- **Data Coverage**: Cost benchmarks, infrastructure data
- **Markets Forecast**: Regional investment trend analysis

### Outcome

Competitiveness analysis reveals:
- Royalty rates 2% above regional average
- Permitting takes 18 months longer than competitor countries
- Infrastructure gap in remote regions adds 15% to project costs

Policy reforms implemented:
- Royalty rate reduced by 1.5%
- Fast-track permitting introduced (target: 12 months)
- $300M infrastructure investment in mining corridors

### Value Delivered

- Exploration investment recovers to previous levels within 2 years
- 3 new mines committed ($5B total investment)
- 2,000 new mining jobs created

## Use Case 3: Resource Nationalism Risk Assessment

**Persona**: Government & Policymaker
**Objective**: Assess the risk of resource nationalism disrupting supply of key minerals and develop mitigation strategies

### Scenario
A foreign ministry is concerned about increasing resource nationalism in key mineral-producing countries and wants to assess the risk to domestic industry.

### Workflow

1. **Access Supply Chain** to map supply sources for critical imports
2. Identify countries where >50% of supply is concentrated
3. **Access Assets Discovery** to understand ownership structures
4. Review recent policy changes in key producing countries
5. **Access Markets Forecast** to assess alternative supply options
6. Develop risk mitigation strategies

### Lens Metals & Mining Capabilities Used

- **Supply Chain**: Supply concentration mapping, trade flow analysis
- **Assets Discovery**: Ownership data, asset-level information
- **Markets Forecast**: Project pipeline and supply alternatives

### Outcome

Risk assessment identifies:
- **High risk**: Rare earths (80% China), Cobalt (70% DRC)
- **Medium risk**: Lithium (60% Australia/Chile), Nickel (50% Indonesia)
- **Low risk**: Copper (diversified), Aluminium (diversified)

Mitigation strategies:
- Diversify supply through bilateral agreements
- Support domestic exploration and mining
- Invest in recycling and circular economy
- Stockpile critical minerals (6-month strategic reserve)

### Value Delivered

- Strategic reserve established for 5 critical minerals
- Reduced supply disruption risk by 40%
- Bilateral critical minerals agreements signed with 3 countries

## Use Case 4: Mine Closure and Rehabilitation Planning

**Persona**: Government & Policymaker
**Objective**: Plan for the responsible closure of aging coal mines and transition of mining communities

### Scenario
A government needs to plan for the closure of 5 aging coal mines over the next decade, ensuring environmental rehabilitation and economic transition for affected communities.

### Workflow

1. **Access Markets Forecast** for coal long-term demand outlook
2. Assess mine economics under declining demand scenarios
3. **Access Assets Discovery** for reserve data and closure timelines
4. Estimate rehabilitation costs and bond requirements
5. Identify alternative economic opportunities in each region
6. Develop transition plans

### Lens Metals & Mining Capabilities Used

- **Markets Forecast**: Coal demand scenarios to 2050
- **Assets Discovery**: Asset-level production, reserve, and cost data
- **Markets Scenarios**: Energy transition impact on coal demand

### Outcome

Transition plan includes:
- Phased closure schedule over 10 years (avoiding community shock)
- $200M rehabilitation fund established
- Critical minerals exploration promoted in coal regions
- Retraining programs for 3,000 workers

### Value Delivered

- No involuntary redundancies (managed through natural attrition + retraining)
- Environmental liabilities fully funded
- 2 new critical minerals discoveries in former coal regions
- Community support maintained throughout transition

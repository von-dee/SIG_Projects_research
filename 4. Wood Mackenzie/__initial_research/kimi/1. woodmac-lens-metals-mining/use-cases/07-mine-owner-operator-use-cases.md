## Use Case 1: Cost Reduction Target Setting

**Persona**: Mine Owner & Operator
**Objective**: Set realistic but ambitious cost reduction targets by benchmarking against global peers

### Scenario
The Operations Director of a mid-tier gold mine needs to reduce AISC by 10% over 2 years. Before setting targets, they need to understand how the mine's costs compare to similar operations globally.

### Workflow

1. **Access Assets Discovery** to identify peer gold mines (similar scale, jurisdiction, ore type)
2. Compare AISC breakdown (mining, processing, G&A, royalties, sustaining capital)
3. Identify specific cost categories where the mine is above peer average
4. Set targets for each cost category based on peer benchmarks
5. Develop improvement initiatives for underperforming areas
6. Track progress quarterly

### Lens Metals & Mining Capabilities Used

- **Assets Discovery**: Peer benchmarking by commodity, scale, and jurisdiction
- **Data Coverage**: AISC breakdowns, operational benchmarks (productivity, energy, consumables)

### Outcome

Benchmarking reveals:
- Mining costs 15% above peer average (inefficient haul truck utilisation)
- Processing costs 8% above average (suboptimal grind size)
- G&A 5% below average (good performance)
- Energy costs 12% above average (old equipment)

Target initiatives:
- Mining: Dispatch optimisation system ($2M investment, $4M annual saving)
- Processing: Grinding circuit optimisation ($500K, $2M saving)
- Energy: LED lighting and variable speed drives ($1M, $1.5M saving)

### Value Delivered

- AISC reduced by 12% (exceeding 10% target)
- $7.5M annual cost savings
- Mine moves from 3rd to 2nd quartile on cost curve

## Use Case 2: Expansion Project Feasibility Assessment

**Persona**: Mine Owner & Operator
**Objective**: Evaluate the economics of expanding the processing plant to increase throughput by 25%

### Scenario
A copper mine operator is considering expanding the processing plant from 40,000 tpd to 50,000 tpd. Need to understand the copper price outlook and incentive price to justify the $200M capital investment.

### Workflow

1. **Access Markets Forecast** for copper long-term price outlook to 2050
2. **Access Assets Valuations** to model expansion project economics
3. Input capital cost, increased production, and operating cost assumptions
4. Calculate NPV and IRR under base case and downside price scenarios
5. Determine incentive copper price for the expansion
6. Compare to forecast price range

### Lens Metals & Mining Capabilities Used

- **Markets Forecast**: Copper price forecasts, supply-demand balances
- **Assets Valuations**: Project NPV and IRR modelling
- **Data Coverage**: Capital cost benchmarks for similar expansions

### Outcome

Feasibility results:
- Base case NPV: $350M (at $3.80/lb long-term copper)
- IRR: 22%
- Incentive price: $2.80/lb (well below forecast)
- Payback period: 3.5 years

Decision: Project approved

### Value Delivered

- $350M value creation from expansion
- Additional 50,000 tonnes copper annually
- IRR exceeds company hurdle rate of 15%

## Use Case 3: Reserve Replacement Strategy

**Persona**: Mine Owner & Operator
**Objective**: Develop a strategy to replace depleting reserves and extend mine life

### Scenario
A zinc-lead mine has 5 years of reserves remaining. The Geology Manager needs to identify near-mine exploration targets and assess inorganic growth opportunities.

### Workflow

1. **Access Assets Discovery** to identify nearby zinc-lead projects and mines
2. Map geological potential within 50km radius
3. Assess project pipeline for acquisition or JV opportunities
4. **Access Markets Forecast** for zinc-lead long-term outlook
5. Rank opportunities by proximity, grade, and economics
6. Present options to management

### Lens Metals & Mining Capabilities Used

- **Assets Discovery**: Project pipeline, reserve data, ownership information
- **Markets Forecast**: Zinc-lead demand and price outlook
- **Data Coverage**: Cost curve positioning for potential acquisitions

### Outcome

Options identified:
- **Option 1**: Brownfields exploration ($20M, potential 3-year mine life extension)
- **Option 2**: Acquire adjacent project ($80M, adds 8 years)
- **Option 3**: Joint venture on nearby exploration ground ($10M earn-in)

Decision: Pursue Option 1 immediately + Option 3 JV
- Extends mine life to 10+ years
- Total investment: $30M vs. $80M for acquisition

### Value Delivered

- $50M capital saved vs. full acquisition
- Mine life extended to 10+ years
- Maintained operational continuity and workforce

## Use Case 4: Energy Transition Impact Assessment

**Persona**: Mine Owner & Operator
**Objective**: Assess how decarbonisation policies and carbon pricing will affect the operation's cost structure and competitiveness

### Scenario
A coal mine operator needs to understand the impact of proposed carbon border adjustment mechanisms (CBAM) on export competitiveness and plan for transition.

### Workflow

1. **Access Markets Forecast** for thermal coal demand under different policy scenarios
2. **Access Markets Scenarios** for carbon price trajectories
3. Calculate CBAM cost impact on exports to Europe
4. Compare to competitor countries' carbon intensity
5. Assess cost of decarbonisation options
6. Develop transition strategy

### Lens Metals & Mining Capabilities Used

- **Markets Forecast**: Coal demand scenarios, carbon price forecasts
- **Markets Scenarios**: Policy scenario analysis
- **Data Coverage**: Production costs, carbon intensity benchmarks

### Outcome

Impact assessment:
- CBAM adds $15/t to export cost to EU (10% cost increase)
- Mine remains competitive but margin compressed
- Demand declining under all scenarios post-2035

Transition plan:
- Investigate rare earth potential in surrounding tenements
- Plan workforce transition program
- Phase production down over 10 years

### Value Delivered

- Avoided $50M stranded asset risk through proactive planning
- Identified rare earth exploration potential (new revenue stream)
- Managed workforce transition responsibly

## Use Case 1: Critical Mineral Supply Risk Assessment

**Persona**: Supply Chain & Procurement Manager
**Objective**: Identify and mitigate supply chain risks for critical raw materials used in manufacturing

### Scenario
An automotive manufacturer is ramping up EV production and needs to secure long-term supply of lithium, cobalt, and nickel for battery cells. The procurement team must assess supply risks and develop mitigation strategies.

### Workflow

1. **Access Supply Chain** to map current supply sources for each material
2. Identify geographic and supplier concentration risks
3. **Access Markets Forecast** for demand-supply balances to 2035
4. Calculate supply deficit/surplus for each material
5. Identify alternative supply sources
6. Develop dual-sourcing strategy

### Lens Metals & Mining Capabilities Used

- **Supply Chain**: Material flow mapping, concentration risk analysis
- **Markets Forecast**: Long-term supply-demand forecasts
- **Assets Discovery**: Global supply source database

### Outcome

Risk assessment:
- **Cobalt**: 65% from DRC (high risk) - diversify to Indonesian and Australian sources
- **Lithium**: 55% from Australia/Chile (medium risk) - add Argentine source
- **Nickel**: 50% from Indonesia (medium risk) - add Canadian and New Caledonia sources

Mitigation actions:
- Dual-source all critical materials by 2026
- Establish 90-day strategic inventory for cobalt
- Sign offtake agreements with 2 suppliers per material

### Value Delivered

- Reduced supply disruption risk by 60%
- Avoided $200M potential production loss from supply interruption
- Secured supply at pre-rally prices (saving $50M annually)

## Use Case 2: Raw Material Price Forecasting for Budget Planning

**Persona**: Supply Chain & Procurement Manager
**Objective**: Develop accurate raw material cost assumptions for annual budget and long-term financial planning

### Scenario
The procurement director needs to provide the CFO with 3-year and 10-year cost assumptions for copper, aluminium, and zinc used in manufacturing operations.

### Workflow

1. **Access Markets Forecast** for copper, aluminium, and zinc price forecasts
2. Extract short-term (2-year monthly) and long-term (to 2050) forecasts
3. Review historical price accuracy of forecasts
4. Adjust for contract terms (discounts, premia, TC/RCs)
5. Build cost assumptions by year
6. Present to CFO with confidence intervals

### Lens Metals & Mining Capabilities Used

- **Markets Forecast**: Price forecasts (short and long-term), historical data
- **Data Coverage**: Treatment charges, refining charges, premium/discount data

### Outcome

Budget assumptions:
- Year 1: Copper $4.00/lb, Aluminium $2,200/t, Zinc $2,500/t
- Year 2: Copper $3.80/lb, Aluminium $2,300/t, Zinc $2,400/t
- Year 3: Copper $3.70/lb, Aluminium $2,400/t, Zinc $2,300/t

Budget accuracy:
- Actual Year 1 costs within 5% of forecast
- Working capital optimised based on price trajectory
- Procurement hedging strategy aligned with price outlook

### Value Delivered

- Budget variance reduced from 15% to 5%
- $10M working capital optimisation
- Procurement team credibility enhanced with Finance

## Use Case 3: Supplier Sustainability Audit

**Persona**: Supply Chain & Procurement Manager
**Objective**: Verify responsible sourcing claims from cobalt suppliers and ensure ESG compliance

### Scenario
The company's customers (major electronics brands) require proof of responsible cobalt sourcing, including no child labour and no conflict minerals. The procurement team needs to audit the supply chain back to the mine.

### Workflow

1. **Access Supply Chain** to trace cobalt supply chain from mine to refiner
2. Map supplier locations and ownership structures
3. Cross-reference with known high-risk areas
4. Identify gaps in supply chain visibility
5. Develop supplier audit program
6. Implement supply chain certification requirements

### Lens Metals & Mining Capabilities Used

- **Supply Chain**: Material flow tracking, asset location data
- **Assets Discovery**: Ownership structures, asset-level data
- **Data Coverage**: Jurisdiction risk assessments

### Outcome

Supply chain audit reveals:
- 70% of cobalt traceable to mine level
- 20% from artisanal sources (high risk)
- 10% with unknown provenance

Actions taken:
- Switched 20% artisanal supply to certified large-scale mines
- Implemented blockchain traceability for remaining 10%
- Achieved 100% traceability within 12 months

### Value Delivered

- Customer sustainability requirements met
- Maintained supply contracts worth $500M annually
- Brand reputation protected
- Premium pricing for certified sustainable products

## Use Case 4: Strategic Inventory Decision

**Persona**: Supply Chain & Procurement Manager
**Objective**: Determine optimal inventory levels for nickel given market volatility and supply uncertainty

### Scenario
Nickel prices have been volatile following the LME nickel squeeze. The procurement team must decide whether to increase inventory, decrease it, or maintain current levels.

### Workflow

1. **Access Markets Forecast** for nickel short-term outlook
2. Analyse supply-demand balance and inventory levels
3. Review project pipeline for supply additions
4. Assess demand from EV battery sector
5. **Access Markets Scenarios** for different price scenarios
6. Model inventory holding costs vs. price risk

### Lens Metals & Mining Capabilities Used

- **Markets Forecast**: Nickel supply-demand, inventory data, price outlook
- **Markets Scenarios**: Price scenario analysis
- **Assets Discovery**: Project pipeline tracking

### Outcome

Analysis shows:
- Market moving into deficit as Indonesian supply growth slows
- EV demand accelerating faster than supply response
- Price volatility likely to persist

Decision:
- Maintain 60-day inventory (vs. standard 30 days)
- Lock in 50% of annual requirement via long-term contract at $22,000/t
- Remain 50% spot for flexibility

### Value Delivered

- Avoided $30M additional cost from spot price spike
- Supply security maintained during volatile period
- Average purchase price $3,000/t below peak spot price

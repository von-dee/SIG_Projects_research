# User Persona: Government & Policymaker

## Persona Overview

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Role** | Policy Advisor / Critical Minerals Director / Mining Regulator |
| **Industry** | Government / Public Sector / International Organisations |
| **Experience Level** | 10-20 years |
| **Reports To** | Minister / Department Head / Agency Director |
| **Direct Reports** | Policy analysts, research staff |

## Demographics

- **Age Range**: 35-55
- **Education**: Public Policy, Economics, Law, or Engineering; advanced degree common
- **Location**: Capital cities, regional mining jurisdictions
- **Work Style**: Research-intensive, stakeholder-focused, policy-oriented

## Goals & Objectives

1. **Supply Chain Security**: Ensure secure access to critical minerals for domestic industry
2. **Policy Development**: Create evidence-based policies for the mining sector
3. **Economic Development**: Attract mining investment and maximise economic benefits
4. **Regulatory Oversight**: Ensure responsible mining practices and environmental compliance
5. **Strategic Reserves**: Assess the need for and manage strategic mineral reserves
6. **International Engagement**: Participate in international forums on critical minerals

## Pain Points

1. **Data Access**: Limited access to comprehensive, independent market data
2. **Complexity**: Mining markets are technically complex and difficult to analyse
3. **Rapid Change**: Technology and market dynamics evolve faster than policy cycles
4. **Geopolitical Risk**: Concentration of supply in potentially unstable regions
5. **Coordination**: Need to coordinate across multiple government departments
6. **Evidence Base**: Pressure to justify policy decisions with robust evidence

## Technology Profile

- Uses standard office productivity tools
- Needs accessible, visual data presentations
- Requires printable reports and briefings
- Works with GIS for spatial analysis
- Needs data export for further analysis

## Key Questions This Persona Needs Answered

1. How dependent are we on imports for critical minerals?
2. What is the global competitive landscape for mining investment?
3. How will energy transition affect demand for domestically produced minerals?
4. What supply chain vulnerabilities exist in our key industrial sectors?
5. How do our mining policies compare to competitor jurisdictions?
6. What minerals are truly "critical" based on supply risk and economic importance?

## Preferred Solutions

- **Supply Chain**: Supply chain resilience analysis and material flow mapping
- **Assets Discovery**: Benchmark mining assets globally for competitiveness analysis
- **Markets Forecast**: Long-term demand forecasts for policy planning
- **Markets Scenarios**: Energy transition scenario analysis for strategic planning

## Quote

> "My job is to ensure my country's economic security in an era of rapid technological change. I need reliable, independent analysis of global mineral supply chains to inform policy decisions that will matter for decades."

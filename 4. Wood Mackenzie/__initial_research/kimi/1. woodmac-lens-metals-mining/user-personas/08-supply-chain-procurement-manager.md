# User Persona: Supply Chain & Procurement Manager

## Persona Overview

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Role** | Procurement Manager / Supply Chain Director / Strategic Sourcing Lead |
| **Industry** | Manufacturing / Automotive / Electronics / Energy / Defence |
| **Experience Level** | 8-18 years |
| **Reports To** | COO / VP Supply Chain / Chief Procurement Officer |
| **Direct Reports** | Procurement team, logistics coordinators, supplier quality |

## Demographics

- **Age Range**: 32-50
- **Education**: Supply Chain Management, Business, Engineering, or Logistics
- **Location**: Corporate offices, manufacturing hubs, or regional procurement centres
- **Work Style**: Detail-oriented, process-driven, relationship-focused, risk-aware

## Goals & Objectives

1. **Supply Security**: Ensure reliable, uninterrupted supply of critical raw materials
2. **Cost Management**: Optimise total cost of ownership for procured materials
3. **Risk Mitigation**: Identify and mitigate supply chain risks (concentration, geopolitical, operational)
4. **Supplier Diversification**: Build resilient supplier portfolios across geographies
5. **ESG Compliance**: Ensure suppliers meet environmental and social standards
6. **Inventory Optimisation**: Balance working capital with supply security

## Pain Points

1. **Supply Concentration**: Over-reliance on single suppliers or regions (e.g., China for rare earths)
2. **Price Volatility**: Commodity price swings make budgeting and cost management difficult
3. **Geopolitical Risk**: Trade tensions, sanctions, and resource nationalism disrupt supply
4. **Visibility**: Limited visibility into upstream supply chains beyond Tier 1 suppliers
5. **Quality Consistency**: Variability in raw material quality affects manufacturing processes
6. **Sustainability Pressure**: Increasing requirements to prove responsible sourcing

## Technology Profile

- Uses ERP systems (SAP, Oracle) for procurement processes
- Familiar with supplier management platforms
- Uses Excel for analysis and reporting
- Needs supply chain mapping and risk assessment tools
- Requires alert systems for supply disruptions

## Key Questions This Persona Needs Answered

1. Where are my critical materials sourced from and what are the risks?
2. Which suppliers or regions represent concentration risk?
3. How will energy transition affect availability of battery and critical minerals?
4. What alternative supply sources exist if primary sources are disrupted?
5. What is the long-term price outlook for my key raw materials?
6. How can I trace the supply chain back to the mine for ESG compliance?

## Preferred Solutions

- **Supply Chain**: Material flow tracking from mines to refineries, risk assessment
- **Markets Forecast**: Long-term supply and price forecasts for procurement planning
- **Assets Discovery**: Map global supply sources and identify alternatives
- **Markets Scenarios**: Assess how different scenarios affect material availability

## Quote

> "My job is to ensure my company never runs out of the materials we need, at the right price, from responsible sources. I need to see upstream of my suppliers to understand where the risks are hiding."

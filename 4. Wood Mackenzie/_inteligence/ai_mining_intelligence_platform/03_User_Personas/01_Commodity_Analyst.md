## Background
Sarah spent 5 years at a bulge-bracket bank covering base metals before moving to a hedge fund. She produces weekly research notes on copper, lithium, and nickel markets for portfolio managers. She subscribes to Wood Mackenzie and S&P Global but finds their data stale and their interfaces frustrating.

## Daily Workflow
1. **6:00 AM**: Review overnight price movements on Bloomberg
2. **7:00 AM**: Read overnight news, check email for analyst reports
3. **8:30 AM**: Morning meeting — present market update to PMs
4. **10:00 AM–4:00 PM**: Deep research, model updates, client calls
5. **4:00 PM**: Write daily note, update positions

## Pain Points
- Reports from WM/SPGI arrive on a schedule, not when she needs them
- Cost curve data is 3–6 months old by the time she gets it
- Cannot easily compare her own models with industry benchmarks
- Alerting is manual — she has to set up Google Alerts and monitor Twitter
- PDF reports are not searchable or interactive

## Goals
- Get real-time answers to ad-hoc questions during meetings
- Maintain always-current cost curves and supply/demand models
- Receive proactive alerts when market conditions change
- Benchmark her internal models against industry data

## Technology Profile
- **Primary Tools**: Bloomberg Terminal, Excel (heavy user), Python (basic), Tableau
- **Communication**: Slack, Teams, email
- **Mobile Usage**: Heavy — checks prices and news on phone constantly
- **AI Comfort**: High — uses ChatGPT for drafting, wants AI in workflow tools

## Success Metrics
- Time to answer a research question: < 2 minutes
- Cost curve update frequency: Daily
- Alert response time: < 15 minutes
- Report production time: Cut by 50%

## Quote
> "I need to know *now* whether a water shortage in Chile will affect Q3 copper supply. Waiting 3 days for an analyst report kills my edge."

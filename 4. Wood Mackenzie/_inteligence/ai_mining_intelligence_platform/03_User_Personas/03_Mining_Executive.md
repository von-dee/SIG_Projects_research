## Background
Elena leads corporate strategy, M&A, and investor relations. She needs to understand how her company's assets compare to global peers, track competitor activity, and identify acquisition targets. She uses Wood Mackenzie for benchmarking but finds it expensive and inflexible.

## Daily Workflow
1. **7:00 AM**: Review overnight market and operational updates
2. **8:00 AM**: Executive committee meeting
3. **10:00 AM–12:00 PM**: Strategic planning, M&A evaluation
4. **2:00 PM**: Investor calls or board prep
5. **4:00 PM**: Review internal reports and external intelligence

## Pain Points
- Benchmarking data is outdated and doesn't reflect current cost structures
- Competitor intelligence is scattered across multiple sources
- No easy way to model M&A scenarios with real-time data
- ESG reporting requires manual data collection from multiple sources
- Board presentations require weeks of data gathering

## Goals
- Benchmark her mines against global cost curves in real time
- Monitor competitor production, costs, and strategic moves automatically
- Generate board-ready presentations with one click
- Track ESG incidents and compliance across her portfolio
- Evaluate M&A targets with integrated financial and operational data

## Technology Profile
- **Primary Tools**: PowerPoint, Excel, internal ERP, SAP
- **Communication**: Email, Teams, phone
- **Mobile Usage**: Moderate — checks dashboards on tablet
- **AI Comfort**: Low-Medium — wants AI to augment, not replace, her team

## Success Metrics
- Time to prepare board presentation: Cut from 2 weeks to 2 days
- Competitor intelligence update frequency: Daily
- Cost benchmarking accuracy: Within 5% of actuals
- M&A target evaluation time: Cut from 3 months to 3 weeks

## Quote
> "I need to know if a junior explorer 200km from our flagship asset just hit high-grade ore before my competitors do. Information arbitrage is everything in M&A."

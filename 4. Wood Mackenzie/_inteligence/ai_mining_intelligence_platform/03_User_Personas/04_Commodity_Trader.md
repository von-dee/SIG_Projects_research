## Background
Jimmy trades physical copper cathodes and lithium concentrate. He needs real-time intelligence on supply disruptions, shipping flows, and inventory levels to make split-second trading decisions. He currently pays for multiple data services (Fastmarkets, SMM, Platts) but none talk to each other.

## Daily Workflow
1. **5:00 AM**: Check overnight LME/SHFE closes, shipping data
2. **6:00 AM**: Morning call with London desk
3. **7:00 AM–6:00 PM**: Trading, negotiating, hedging, logistics
4. **Evening**: Review positions, plan next day

## Pain Points
- AIS shipping data is raw and requires manual interpretation
- Inventory data (LME, SHFE warehouses) is delayed by days
- No single source for "what's happening right now" in the physical market
- Price assessments from PRAs are subjective and delayed
- Cannot easily correlate news events with price movements

## Goals
- Real-time visibility into global shipping flows for key commodities
- Instant correlation between news events and price impact
- Automated inventory tracking across major warehouses
- Predictive alerts for supply disruptions before they hit the market

## Technology Profile
- **Primary Tools**: Bloomberg, Eikon, internal trading system, Excel
- **Communication**: Bloomberg IB, WhatsApp, phone
- **Mobile Usage**: Critical — trades on mobile, needs push alerts
- **AI Comfort**: High — early adopter of any tool that gives an edge

## Success Metrics
- Time to identify supply disruption: < 30 minutes from event
- Shipping flow visibility: Real-time, global
- Price prediction accuracy: > 70% for 24-hour moves
- Trade decision speed: Cut by 50%

## Quote
> "If I know a Chilean port is closed due to a strike 2 hours before the market does, I make money. If I find out from the news, I'm too late."

## Background
Priya advises mining companies and investors on ESG compliance, TCFD reporting, and responsible sourcing. She needs comprehensive ESG data on mining assets globally — incidents, certifications, water usage, community relations, tailings dam status. Currently, she manually compiles this from scattered sources.

## Daily Workflow
1. **8:00 AM**: Review overnight ESG news and incident reports
2. **9:00 AM**: Client calls and project work
3. **12:00 PM**: Data collection and analysis
4. **2:00 PM**: Report writing and presentation prep
5. **4:00 PM**: Team coordination and knowledge sharing

## Pain Points
- ESG data on mining assets is fragmented across NGOs, government reports, and company disclosures
- No standardized ESG scoring for mining-specific risks
- Tailings dam incidents, labor strikes, and community conflicts are reported too late
- Cannot easily benchmark clients against peers on ESG metrics
- Regulatory reporting (EU Taxonomy, SEC climate rules) requires massive manual effort

## Goals
- Access a unified ESG database for all mining assets globally
- Receive real-time alerts for ESG incidents at client or competitor assets
- Generate ESG benchmarking reports with one click
- Track regulatory compliance across jurisdictions
- Build custom ESG scoring models

## Technology Profile
- **Primary Tools**: Excel, PowerPoint, internal databases, GRI standards
- **Communication**: Teams, email, client portals
- **Mobile Usage**: Low-Moderate
- **AI Comfort**: Medium — wants AI to reduce manual data entry

## Success Metrics
- Time to compile ESG profile for a new asset: Cut from 2 days to 2 hours
- ESG incident alert latency: < 1 hour from public reporting
- Benchmarking report generation: < 1 day
- Regulatory compliance tracking: Automated

## Quote
> "My clients' investors are asking for ESG data we don't have. I spend 60% of my time collecting data and 40% analyzing it. That ratio should be reversed."

## Background
David leads M&A and capital markets advisory for mining clients across APAC. He needs comprehensive asset-level data for pitch books, valuation models, and due diligence. He currently pays $200K+/year for Wood Mackenzie and S&P Global subscriptions but still has his analysts manually build models.

## Daily Workflow
1. **6:00 AM**: Market update, deal pipeline review
2. **7:30 AM**: Morning team meeting
3. **9:00 AM–6:00 PM**: Client meetings, pitch preparation, due diligence
4. **Evening**: Review materials, sign off on models

## Pain Points
- Building a pitch book requires weeks of manual data collection
- Comparable company analysis is outdated by the time it's presented
- No easy way to model commodity price scenarios in valuation
- Due diligence data rooms lack mining-specific intelligence
- Cannot easily track which assets are "in play" for M&A

## Goals
- Generate pitch book data packs in hours, not weeks
- Access real-time comparable company and transaction data
- Run commodity price sensitivity analyses instantly
- Track M&A activity and rumors across the sector
- Build credible valuation models with verified data

## Technology Profile
- **Primary Tools**: Excel (power user), PowerPoint, Bloomberg, CapIQ
- **Communication**: Bloomberg IB, email, phone
- **Mobile Usage**: Moderate — reviews decks on tablet
- **AI Comfort**: Medium — wants accuracy and auditability above all

## Success Metrics
- Pitch book production time: Cut from 3 weeks to 3 days
- Valuation model accuracy: Within 10% of final transaction price
- M&A pipeline visibility: Real-time tracking
- Due diligence efficiency: Cut by 40%

## Quote
> "My analysts spend 80 hours building a comparable company table that should take 8 minutes. If I can automate that, I can redeploy them to actually thinking about the deal."

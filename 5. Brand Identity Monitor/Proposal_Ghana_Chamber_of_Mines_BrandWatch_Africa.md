# Proposal to the Ghana Chamber of Mines

## BrandWatch Africa: AI-Powered Reputation, Community Sentiment, and ESG Intelligence for Ghana's Mining Sector

**Prepared for:** The Ghana Chamber of Mines  
**Prepared by:** BrandWatch Africa / SIGNAL Reputation Intelligence Platform  
**Date:** June 2026  
**Proposed engagement:** Chamber-led pilot and sector intelligence partnership

## Executive Summary

Ghana's mining industry operates in a high-trust, high-scrutiny environment. Member companies are expected to maintain strong community relationships, demonstrate responsible environmental and social performance, respond quickly to misinformation, and provide credible evidence of social license to operate. Yet many of the most important reputation signals are still fragmented across radio discussions, local-language conversations, community reports, online news, social media, stakeholder meetings, and field officer notes.

BrandWatch Africa is proposed as a Chamber-led reputation and community sentiment intelligence platform for the mining sector. The platform combines multi-channel monitoring, Ghanaian and African language sentiment analysis, stakeholder-specific risk scoring, narrative tracking, and ESG-ready reporting. It is designed to help the Ghana Chamber of Mines and its member companies move from reactive reputation management to earlier detection, stronger evidence, and coordinated action.

The core promise is simple: instead of only knowing that mining is being discussed, the Chamber and its members will know which communities are losing trust, which issues are driving sentiment, where narratives are spreading, and what action is needed before concerns escalate into reputational, regulatory, or operational risk.

We recommend an initial 8 to 12 week pilot focused on one or more mining corridors, selected member companies, and sector-wide narratives. The pilot would produce a live intelligence dashboard, weekly insight briefings, issue and risk maps, a final executive report, and a clear roadmap for expanding the system across Chamber members.

## Why This Matters Now

Mining companies in Ghana face a convergence of social, regulatory, investor, and communications pressures. Community expectations around participation, compensation, jobs, environmental protection, local content, and sustainable development continue to rise. ESG reporting expectations require stronger evidence, not only anecdotes. Media narratives can shift quickly, and industry-wide perception can be affected by incidents involving a single company or by misinformation connected to illegal mining, environmental harm, safety, or community grievances.

Existing global social listening tools are not built for this context. They often emphasize online mentions, English-language social media, and generic sentiment labels. They can miss the channels and language patterns that matter most in Ghanaian mining communities, including local radio, community reports, traditional stakeholder concerns, code-switching, Ghanaian English, Twi, Akan, Ga, Ewe, Dagbani, Hausa, and French-language regional content.

For the Chamber, this creates an opportunity to lead. A sector-level reputation intelligence system can help the Chamber understand the health of the industry's public standing, detect emerging issues early, support member companies with better insight, and strengthen engagement with government, regulators, host communities, investors, media, and civil society.

## Proposed Solution

BrandWatch Africa is an AI-powered brand identity and reputation monitoring platform built for African markets and high-impact sectors. For the Ghana Chamber of Mines, the platform would be configured as a mining-sector intelligence system with four connected capabilities.

### 1. Multi-Channel Monitoring

The platform monitors and consolidates signals from channels that are relevant to mining-sector reputation:

- Online news, blogs, and public web sources
- Social media posts and comments where compliant access is available
- Radio transcripts and audio-derived text where available
- Public regulator and government statements
- Uploaded field reports, survey exports, call center logs, or community relations notes
- Stakeholder and community feedback datasets provided by member companies
- Competitor and sector-wide references relevant to mining reputation

This creates a single source of truth for reputation signals that currently sit across disconnected tools, spreadsheets, reports, and media tracking processes.

### 2. Local Context Sentiment and Narrative Intelligence

The sentiment engine classifies mentions as positive, negative, neutral, mixed, or uncertain while accounting for Ghanaian and wider African communication patterns. The platform is designed to support English and local-language sentiment detection, code-switching, sarcasm, indirect criticism, praise-with-complaint, community grievance language, and culturally specific expressions.

Beyond sentiment, the system detects emotion, intent, topic, stakeholder group, and location. This allows the Chamber to distinguish between different types of concern, such as employment requests, compensation issues, environmental concerns, safety complaints, regulatory concerns, misinformation, media inquiries, or protest signals.

### 3. Community and Sector Risk Scoring

BrandWatch Africa converts raw mentions into prioritized risk signals. Each issue can receive a risk score based on sentiment intensity, mention volume change, source credibility, spread velocity, topic severity, location, stakeholder group, and whether similar issues have previously escalated.

This is especially important for mining because not all negative mentions carry the same operational meaning. An isolated complaint requires monitoring. A fast-growing narrative about water pollution, land compensation, worker safety, youth unemployment, or regulator action may require immediate escalation.

### 4. ESG and Executive Reporting

The platform turns monitoring data into evidence that can support Chamber reports, member briefings, board updates, ESG disclosures, sustainability reviews, and policy engagement. Outputs can include:

- Industry reputation trend reports
- Community trust and social license indicators
- Sentiment by region, site, channel, topic, and stakeholder group
- Before-and-after analysis for engagement initiatives
- Issue heatmaps and narrative timelines
- Competitor and peer benchmarking
- Executive-ready PDF and PowerPoint-style summaries

## Value to the Ghana Chamber of Mines

The proposed platform would give the Chamber an industry-wide intelligence capability rather than a narrow company-specific monitoring tool.

### Sector Reputation Management

The Chamber would gain a clearer view of how mining is being discussed across media, digital, and community channels. This supports proactive communication, more precise public education, and stronger response to narratives that may affect the whole sector.

### Early Warning on Emerging Issues

The system can surface rising concerns before they become national headlines, protests, regulatory scrutiny, or reputational crises. It can help the Chamber identify when a local issue is becoming an industry issue.

### Better Member Support

The Chamber can provide members with evidence-based insight into sentiment trends, recurring concerns, and stakeholder priorities. Over time, anonymized benchmarking can help members compare community trust, issue volume, response times, and improvement trends.

### ESG and Social License Evidence

The platform gives the Chamber and its members more measurable evidence for ESG reporting, investor engagement, sustainability narratives, and responsible mining commitments. It can help translate community perception into a trackable social license indicator.

### Misinformation and Narrative Response

When false or misleading narratives emerge, the Chamber can detect them earlier, understand where they are spreading, and coordinate factual, context-aware responses with members and stakeholders.

## Value to Member Companies

Member companies would benefit from practical operational intelligence, not only dashboards. Key use cases include:

- Detecting community grievances before they escalate
- Understanding which topics drive trust or distrust around specific sites
- Tracking sentiment around jobs, compensation, local content, environment, safety, CSR, and land issues
- Reducing manual time spent compiling media and community reports
- Supporting ESG, board, and investor reporting with more defensible data
- Measuring whether engagement activities improve sentiment over time
- Identifying low-confidence or culturally nuanced mentions for human review
- Benchmarking company reputation against sector trends and peer performance

## Proposed Pilot

We recommend an 8 to 12 week pilot designed to prove value quickly while respecting data access, privacy, member confidentiality, and the Chamber's coordination role.

### Pilot Objectives

The pilot should answer five practical questions:

1. What are the dominant narratives affecting mining-sector reputation in selected Ghanaian markets?
2. Which topics, channels, communities, and stakeholder groups are driving positive and negative sentiment?
3. Which emerging issues show early signs of escalation?
4. How can the Chamber and member companies use sentiment intelligence for ESG reporting and stakeholder engagement?
5. What data sources, workflows, and governance model are needed for a wider sector rollout?

### Suggested Scope

The pilot can be configured in one of three ways:

| Option | Scope | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| Chamber Sector Pulse | Monitor industry-wide mining narratives across selected public sources | Establishing the Chamber's baseline intelligence capability |
| Corridor Pilot | Focus on one mining corridor or region with selected member companies and community topics | Testing community sentiment and local issue detection |
| Member Consortium Pilot | Include a small group of participating member companies with anonymized benchmark reporting | Proving value for member expansion and paid rollout |

### Pilot Data Sources

The initial pilot would prioritize sources that are practical, compliant, and immediately useful:

- Public online news and blogs
- Selected social media and public conversation sources
- Chamber-provided keyword lists and issue taxonomies
- Public regulator and government communications
- Uploaded historical reports or media tracking data where available
- Sample community relations reports or survey exports from participating members
- Radio transcript integrations where available or manually supplied for the pilot

### Pilot Deliverables

The pilot would produce:

- A configured BrandWatch Africa dashboard for Chamber users
- A mining-sector topic taxonomy covering environment, employment, compensation, safety, local content, community investment, illegal mining association risk, regulation, media narratives, and stakeholder trust
- Weekly intelligence briefs highlighting top narratives, sentiment movement, emerging risks, and recommended actions
- A risk queue that prioritizes issues by urgency, location, stakeholder group, and topic
- A human review workflow for low-confidence or culturally ambiguous sentiment
- A final executive report with findings, screenshots, sample dashboards, pilot metrics, and scale-up recommendations
- A rollout plan for expanding to additional regions, member companies, sources, and reporting formats

## Implementation Roadmap

The broader implementation can be delivered in five phases.

### Phase 1: Sentiment MVP

The first phase proves that mentions, media items, and uploaded reports can be transformed into useful business signals. It includes ingestion from CSV uploads, news feeds, and selected social sources; basic language detection; sentiment labels; confidence scoring; topic tags; a basic dashboard; and a manual review workflow.

### Phase 2: Context-Aware Sentiment

The second phase improves relevance for Ghanaian and African communication realities. It adds local-language examples, code-switching support, emotion and intent detection, explainable sentiment output, stakeholder classification, location extraction, and sentiment views by community, region, source, and stakeholder group.

### Phase 3: Risk and Alerts

The third phase turns sentiment into prioritization and action. It includes community risk scoring, alert thresholds, narrative velocity tracking, suggested response owners, escalation workflows, and alerts through email, Slack, SMS, or WhatsApp Business where appropriate.

### Phase 4: ESG and Executive Reporting

The fourth phase makes the platform useful for formal reporting and leadership decisions. It includes ESG-aligned report templates, a social license trend score, community trust index, board-ready exports, competitor benchmarking, intervention impact analysis, and a data export API.

### Phase 5: Enterprise Intelligence Platform

The fifth phase scales the system into a sector intelligence platform. It adds multi-country support, custom model tuning, advanced radio and audio transcription, narrative clustering, bot and synthetic content detection, predictive crisis forecasting, enterprise permissions, audit logs, and managed analyst workflows.

## Governance and Data Protection

Because reputation and community sentiment data can be sensitive, the pilot should include clear governance from the start.

Recommended governance principles:

- Use public, licensed, customer-provided, or ethically collected data sources
- Minimize personal data collection wherever possible
- Redact personally identifiable information in field reports and call center exports where appropriate
- Define role-based access for Chamber users, participating members, and analysts
- Keep audit logs for model overrides, report exports, and sensitive workflow actions
- Separate company-specific views from anonymized sector-wide benchmark views
- Route low-confidence or culturally sensitive classifications for human review
- Agree on a protocol for urgent issue escalation and member notification

## Success Metrics

The pilot should be evaluated against practical outcome measures.

Product performance metrics:

- Sentiment classification agreement with human reviewers
- Percentage of mentions classified with high confidence
- Time from mention ingestion to usable insight
- Number of high-risk issues detected
- Alert precision and usefulness
- Dashboard usage by Chamber and pilot stakeholders

Business and stakeholder outcome metrics:

- Earlier detection of community or media issues
- Reduction in manual reporting time
- Quality of weekly insight briefings
- Usefulness of ESG-ready evidence
- Ability to identify dominant narratives and issue drivers
- Member appetite for expansion after pilot completion

## Commercial Model

We recommend a pilot-first commercial approach.

### Pilot Engagement

The pilot can be structured as a fixed-scope engagement covering setup, taxonomy design, data ingestion, dashboard configuration, weekly analysis, and final reporting. Final pricing should be determined after confirming source volume, number of users, member participation, and whether radio transcription is included.

### Scale-Up Options

After the pilot, the Chamber can choose one or more scale-up paths:

- Chamber subscription for sector-wide reputation intelligence
- Member company subscriptions for company-specific dashboards
- Consortium model with participating members sharing base platform costs
- Managed analyst service for monthly or quarterly insight reviews
- Add-on modules for radio monitoring, ESG report automation, crisis dashboards, historical backfill, custom board reports, and API access

## Topic 1: AI Doctors — Would You Trust a Diagnosis From a Machine?

### The Big Picture

Artificial Intelligence is rapidly advancing in healthcare. However, major studies reveal a massive gap between a machine's book-smarts and real-world medical expertise. A 2025 meta-analysis in *Nature Digital Medicine* found that AI chatbots have an overall diagnostic accuracy of just **52.1%** (comparable to a coin toss) and trail expert specialist physicians by **15.8%**. Despite this, millions of people use platforms like ChatGPT daily for health advice, leading major health organizations to name AI chatbot misuse a top patient safety concern.

### Key Talking Points for the Presenter

* **The Diagnostic Gap:** While AI can pass medical exams on paper, real diagnosing requires reading body language, hearing a patient’s tone, and years of clinical intuition.
* **The Danger of "Self-Diagnosing":** Nearly 1 in 3 people say they would skip seeing a real doctor if an AI tool classified their symptoms as "low risk". This could lead to serious medical conditions being ignored.
* **The "Hallucination" Risk:** Medical watchdogs have documented instances where AI models confidently invented non-existent body parts or suggested dangerous device placements.
* **The Best-Case Scenario:** AI shouldn't *replace* doctors; it should *assist* them. When a human doctor uses AI to cross-reference data, the results are highly accurate.

### On-Screen Graphics / Production Ideas

* **Headline Banner:** *AI Doctors vs. Human Touch: Coin Flip or Cure?*
* **Stat Pop-up:** *AI overall diagnostic accuracy: 52% (Nature Digital Medicine Study)*.
* **Social Media Poll:** *"Would you let an AI app write your prescription? (Yes / No)"*

## Topic 2: The End of the "Human" Influencer? The Rise of AI Stars

## Topic 3: AI Cupid — Is Using AI to Find Love Genius or "Chatfishing"?

### The Big Picture

The dating world has a new term: **"Chatfishing"**. This is the practice of using AI "wingman" apps (like *Wingman AI*, *Winggg*, or *Casanova*) to craft witty pick-up lines, keep conversations going, or rewrite online dating bios. AI usage among singles has spiked dramatically, with nearly half of Gen Z dating app users turning to AI for romantic help. The ultimate catch? When you finally meet in person, the "personality plagiarism" is exposed, and the real-life conversation can't match the artificial wit.

### Key Talking Points for the Presenter

* **Dating App Rizz-on-Demand:** Apps like *Winggg* analyze a match's profile to create personalized, clever openers so you don't have to overthink your messages.
* **The "Personality Plagiarism" Trap:** If your online humor is generated by an AI model, are you setting yourself up to fail when you finally meet face-to-face?
* **AI as a Relationship Therapist:** People are now uploading screenshots of their private WhatsApp or DM chats to AI tools to analyze their crush’s "attachment style" or look for red flags.

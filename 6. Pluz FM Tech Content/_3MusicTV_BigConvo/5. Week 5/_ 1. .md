
## 1. "AI Just Applied for Your Job — Did It Get the Offer?"

(Alts: "Fired by a Robot: Is Your Job Ghana-Proof or AI-Proof?" / "The Great Job Heist: How AI Is Quietly Stealing Ghana's Workforce")

Which jobs in Ghana and beyond are already getting eaten by AI — from call centres to graphic design — and whether it's time to panic or pivot.

Notes:

- Ghana currently has no national AI workforce strategy — no published guidelines on retraining funds or employer obligations when jobs get automated. Good contrast point.
- Compare with neighbours: Kenya launched a National AI and Automation Workforce Fund in 2024 (roughly GHS 100m equivalent for reskilling), and Nigeria runs free AI literacy bootcamps in Lagos, Abuja, and Port Harcourt.
- Highest near-term risk: data entry, basic customer service, junior accounting. Lowest risk through 2030: jobs needing physical presence, cultural judgment, negotiation.
- A stat worth throwing out live: Ghana's informal sector is ~60% of employment and largely insulated from AI displacement short-term — real safety, or just delayed disruption?

How recommendation engines, chatbots, and personal AI assistants build eerily accurate psychological profiles — sometimes predicting what you want before you do.

## 3. "Can a Robot Drop an Afrobeats Banger? AI vs. the Culture"

(Alts: "Ghost Producer: Is AI the New Ghostwriter of Afrobeats?" / "Beat Machine vs. Beatmaker: AI's Takeover of the Studio")

A perfect fit for 3Music's audience: can AI actually make a chart-topping Afrobeats or Highlife record, and what does that mean for producers, songwriters, and "vibe" as an art form?

Notes:

- AI composition tools can now analyse the polyrhythms underneath Afrobeats — layering multiple percussion patterns simultaneously — and generate club-ready instrumentals in seconds instead of the hours it used to take to program by hand.
- There's already a pan-African AI-music movement: the Wits AI and African Music Project (AIAM) brought together artists and engineers from seven countries, including Ghana's own Emmanuel Apetsi, an AI/ML engineer, to build tools like a "musical digital twin" that responds in real time with voice, rhythm, and storytelling.
- Some platforms are marketing "copyright-safe" AI-generated tracks aimed at solving sampling and licensing headaches — worth debating whether that actually protects artists or just launders originality.
- Great live-debate hook: does AI-assisted production count as "cheating," the same way beat-making software or Auto-Tune once did — or is this just the next tool in the box?
- Bigger stakes for the panel: Ghana's music scene is having a massive global moment right now (Black Sherif, Moliy, King Paluta charting internationally) — what happens to that authenticity premium if AI can mass-produce "African-sounding" music from anywhere in the world?

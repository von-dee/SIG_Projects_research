## **1. TV Broadcasting (Nexstar, Gray, Sinclair, TEGNA, Scripps, Graham, Hearst TV, NPG, DBC)**

### Revenue Stream Breakdown
| Stream | Share | Description |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| **Retransmission Fees** | ~55-60% | Fees paid by cable/satellite/streaming distributors to carry local broadcast signals. Negotiated per-subscriber, per-month. |
| **Local/National Advertising** | ~35-40% | 15-30 second commercial spots sold to local businesses and national brands. |
| **Political Advertising** | Cyclical spike | Every 2 years, political campaigns flood local TV with ads. Can double or triple ad revenue in election years. |
| **Digital/OTT** | ~1-5% | Streaming newscasts, station apps, programmatic digital ads. Growing but still small. |

### The Business Sense
TV broadcasters have undergone a **fundamental revenue shift**. Historically, advertising was 80%+ of revenue. Today, **retransmission fees exceed ad revenue** for most major groups. Here's why this makes business sense:

- **Must-Carry Leverage:** Local broadcast signals are legally required content for cable/satellite providers. Stations negotiate from a position of strength.
- **Scale Economics:** Nexstar owns ~200 stations. Gray owns 180+. Larger groups negotiate higher per-subscriber rates.
- **Political Cyclicality:** Local TV is the #1 medium for political campaigns. In 2024, political ad spending on local broadcast TV exceeded $5 billion. This creates massive cash windfalls every election cycle.
- **The "Dual Revenue" Model:** Unlike pure ad-supported media, broadcasters now have two independent revenue streams (ads + retrans), reducing volatility.

**Key Insight:** Nexstar generated $4.9 billion in revenue (2025), with **59% from distribution fees and 40% from advertising**. Retrans fees are now the dominant revenue driver.

## **2. Radio Broadcasting (Audacy, Alpha Media, Bonneville, Midwest Communications, Townsquare)**

### Revenue Stream Breakdown
| Stream | Share | Description |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| **Broadcast Radio Ads** | ~60-70% | Traditional AM/FM spot advertising by daypart (morning drive = premium). |
| **Digital Audio/Podcasts** | ~20-30% | Streaming ads, podcast sponsorships, dynamic ad insertion. Fastest-growing segment. |
| **Live Events** | ~5-10% | Concerts, festivals, brand activations leveraging station brands. |
| **Marketing Solutions** | ~5% | Digital marketing services for local businesses. |

### The Business Sense
Radio is in a **digital transformation crisis**. The model is shifting from "broadcast towers" to "audio everywhere":

- **Local Intimacy:** Radio still reaches 82% of Americans weekly. Local hosts, weather, traffic, and community events create不可替代 (irreplaceable) local connection that Spotify algorithms can't replicate.
- **Digital Pivot:** Audacy (formerly Entercom) rebranded specifically to signal this shift. Digital audio now represents ~25% of revenue and growing.
- **Events as Differentiator:** Live events create direct consumer relationships and high-margin sponsorship opportunities.
- **The Spotify Threat:** Streaming audio is eating broadcast radio's younger demographics. Radio groups must own digital inventory or become irrelevant.

**Key Insight:** Audacy generated $1.17 billion in 2023 but filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy in 2024 due to $2 billion in debt from the CBS Radio merger. The restructuring reduces debt to ~$350 million, allowing focus on digital transformation.

## **3. Telecom + Media (Bell Media/BCE, Rogers)**

### Revenue Stream Breakdown (Rogers as representative)
| Stream | Share | Description |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| **Wireless** | ~49% | Mobile phone plans, data, 5G services. |
| **Cable/Broadband** | ~36% | Home internet, TV bundles, landline. |
| **Media** | ~15% | Sports broadcasting (Sportsnet, Blue Jays), Citytv, radio, publishing. |

### The Business Sense
In Canada, media is **not a standalone business** — it's a **subscriber retention tool**:

- **Churn Reduction:** Sports content (NHL, MLB, NBA) is the #1 reason Canadians don't cancel cable. Rogers owns the Toronto Blue Jays and Sportsnet specifically to keep subscribers locked in.
- **Bundle Economics:** Wireless + Internet + TV bundles have lower churn than any single service. Media makes the bundle "sticky."
- **Cross-Selling:** Bell Media's Crave streaming service is bundled with Bell wireless plans. Rogers' Sportsnet NOW is bundled with Rogers internet.
- **Advertising Synergy:** Media inventory is sold to wireless/cable advertisers, creating internal demand.

**Key Insight:** Rogers generated $21.7 billion in 2025 revenue. Media is only 15% of revenue but drives disproportionate subscriber retention value. Without Sportsnet, Rogers would lose hundreds of thousands of cable subscribers.

## **4. Diversified Media (Hearst, Paramount, Macquarie)**

### Revenue Stream Breakdown (Hearst as representative)
| Stream | Share | Description |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| **Business Information/Data** | ~35% | Fitch Ratings (credit ratings), Hearst Health, Hearst Ventures. |
| **Television** | ~30% | 30+ local TV stations, retrans fees, A&E Networks stake. |
| **Consumer Media** | ~25% | Magazines (Esquire, Cosmopolitan, Popular Mechanics), digital properties. |
| **Other/Investments** | ~10% | Real estate, venture investments. |

### The Business Sense
Diversification is **risk management as strategy**:

- **Fitch Ratings is the Cash Cow:** Credit rating agencies charge subscription fees to banks, insurers, and asset managers. This is recurring, high-margin revenue that doesn't care about advertising cycles.
- **Media Offsets Data Cyclicality:** When financial markets are down, Fitch suffers but TV political ads surge. When markets are up, Fitch booms but TV is flat.
- **Private Ownership = Long-Term Focus:** Hearst is 100% family-owned (founded 1880). No quarterly earnings pressure means they can invest in 10-year transformations.
- **Paramount's Streaming Bet:** Paramount Global is spending billions on Paramount+ to compete with Netflix/Disney+. Theatrical releases and TV licensing provide cash flow to fund this.

**Key Insight:** For Hearst, Fitch Ratings generates more profit than all television operations combined. The media assets exist partly to maintain brand relevance and diversify cash flow.

## **5. Spanish-Language Media (TelevisaUnivision)**

### Revenue Stream Breakdown
| Stream | Share | Description |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| **Linear TV Advertising** | ~50% | Univision Network, UniMás, local TV ads targeting Hispanic audience. |
| **Global Content Licensing** | ~20% | Telenovelas, series sold to 70+ countries. "La Casa de los Famosos" is a global hit. |
| **ViX Streaming** | ~15% | Spanish-language streaming service. Now profitable. |
| **Radio & Digital** | ~15% | Uforia radio network, digital ads, audio streaming. |

### The Business Sense
TelevisaUnivision has a **cultural monopoly** on the U.S. Hispanic market:

- **Demographic Tailwind:** U.S. Hispanic population is growing 4x faster than non-Hispanic. By 2030, 1 in 4 Americans will be Hispanic.
- **ViX is First-Mover Advantage:** While Netflix/Disney+ add Spanish content, ViX is the only dedicated Spanish-language streamer at scale. It reached profitability faster than any major streaming service.
- **Global IP Factory:** Televisa's Mexico City studios produce 100,000+ hours of content annually. Telenovelas are licensed globally — Turkey, Philippines, Africa all buy Mexican dramas.
- **Language Barrier = Moat:** English-language media can't easily replicate the cultural nuance of Spanish-language content.

**Key Insight:** TelevisaUnivision generated >$4.8 billion in revenue. ViX streaming is the growth engine, but linear TV still provides the cash flow to fund it.

## **6. Media + Ad Tech Hybrid (Entravision)**

### Revenue Stream Breakdown
| Stream | Share | Description |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| **Ad Tech (ATS Platform)** | ~78% | Programmatic advertising platform for mobile apps. |
| **Traditional Media** | ~22% | Local TV ads, retrans fees, digital media. |

### The Business Sense
Entravision is executing a **pivot from broadcaster to ad tech company**:

- **Higher Margins:** Ad tech platforms have 60-70% gross margins vs. 30-40% for broadcasting.
- **Tech Valuation:** Ad tech companies trade at 5-10x revenue multiples. Broadcasters trade at 1-2x. Entravision is re-rating its valuation.
- **ATS Revenue Explosion:** In Q1 2026, ATS revenue was $155 million — up 204% year-over-year. Total company revenue was $197 million.
- **Media as Customer Acquisition:** Traditional media relationships give Entravision access to advertisers who they then migrate to the ATS platform.

**Key Insight:** Entravision's ATS platform revenue TRIPLED in one year. The company is effectively becoming an ad tech platform that happens to own some TV stations.

## **7. Regional Sports Networks (NESN)**

### Revenue Stream Breakdown
| Stream | Share | Description |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| **Subscriber Fees** | ~75% | Per-subscriber fees from cable/satellite (~$300M/year). |
| **Advertising** | ~25% | Premium sports ad inventory (highest CPMs in TV). |

### The Business Sense
RSNs operate on **extortion economics** — but legal extortion:

- **Must-Have Content:** Red Sox and Bruins games are non-negotiable for Boston sports fans. Cable providers MUST carry NESN or lose subscribers.
- **Per-Subscriber Fees:** NESN charges ~$4-5 per subscriber per month. With ~5 million subscribers, that's $300M/year before selling a single ad.
- **Advertising Premium:** Sports ads command 2-3x the CPM of regular TV because live sports is the only content people watch in real-time (no DVR skipping).
- **The Cord-Cutting Threat:** As subscribers drop cable, RSNs are launching direct-to-consumer streaming (NESN 360) to capture cord-cutters.

**Key Insight:** NESN generates ~$300 million annually just from subscriber fees. The Red Sox and Bruins rights create a "tax" on Boston sports fans that is effectively unavoidable.

## **8. Cable/Niche Networks (Game Show Network, Outdoor Channel, Reelz)**

### Revenue Stream Breakdown
| Stream | Share | Description |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| **Subscription Fees** | ~60% | Per-subscriber fees from cable/satellite (MVPDs). |
| **Advertising** | ~30% | Niche audience targeting (e.g., outdoor enthusiasts for Outdoor Channel). |
| **Licensing** | ~10% | Content licensing to FAST/AVOD platforms, international. |

### The Business Sense
Niche networks survive on **loyalty and low costs**:

- **MVPDs Need Variety:** Cable packages need 100+ channels to justify $100+/month. Niche networks fill the "long tail" at low cost.
- **Lower Content Costs:** Game Show Network doesn't pay $10M/episode like HBO. Reelz true crime shows cost a fraction of scripted dramas.
- **Loyal Demographics:** Outdoor Channel viewers are affluent men who buy trucks, boats, and gear. Advertisers pay premium CPMs for this narrow audience.
- **FAST/AVOD Licensing:** Niche content libraries are licensed to free ad-supported streaming (FAST) platforms like Pluto TV, Tubi for incremental revenue.

**Key Insight:** Niche networks generate 60% of revenue from subscription fees and only 30% from ads. Their low content costs make them profitable even with small audiences.

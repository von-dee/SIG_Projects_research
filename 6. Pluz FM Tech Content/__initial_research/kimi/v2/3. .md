## **The Ghana Radio Market Context**

Ghana's media landscape has transformed dramatically since the 1990s:

- **~17 million internet users** (53% of population) — digital is growing fast but radio remains dominant for mass reach
- **Media deregulation (1993)** unleashed a private FM boom — hundreds of stations now compete for ad revenue
- **Major competitor:** Multimedia Group (Joy FM, Adom FM, Hitz FM) — Ghana's largest independent media group with 6 radio brands, 3 TV stations, and 700+ employees
- **Advertising is the MAIN revenue source** for virtually all Ghana radio stations (~80%+)
- **Political season = "cocoa season":** Election cycles create massive ad revenue spikes that stations must be operationally ready to capture
- **Key challenge:** Fierce competition for limited advertising dollars from local businesses

Research on Ghana radio stations in Wa (Upper West Region) found that **advertising is the mainstay** for all stations, with marketing teams actively prospecting for ads. Other revenue streams include sponsorships, NGO project implementation, events, and mast rentals to telecom companies. Many stations struggle financially due to market saturation and high operational costs (electricity, NCA license fees).

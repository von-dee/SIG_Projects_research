### Phase 2: The 90-Day MVP (Prove the Concept)

Do not try to build all 20 ideas at once. Start with a 90-Day Minimum Viable Product (MVP) to prove the model works. 

**The Best 90-Day MVP for Pluzz FM:**
Build a **"Pluzz VIP" Progressive Web App (PWA)**. (A PWA is better than a native app initially because users don't need to visit the Play Store; they can just scan a QR code or click a link, and it installs instantly on their phone—crucial for Ghanaian data limits).

**What the Pluzz VIP PWA Does:**
1.  **Data-Lite Live Stream:** Streams the live radio at low data cost.
2.  **MoMo Paywall:** A premium tier (e.g., 10 GHS/month) that removes ads, allows skipping of songs, and gives access to exclusive uncensored podcast episodes of their top presenters.
3.  **The Airtime Promo Engine:** Pluzz promotes the app on-air constantly: *"Text VIP to 1422 or visit pluzzvip.com to stand a chance to win 500 GHS MTN MoMo!"* To enter, users must register with their phone number and MoMo details.

**The 90-Day Goal:** Prove that Pluzz listeners will actually download the app, use it, and pay MoMo money into it. Once you prove this, you unlock the big budgets.

### Phase 3: Structuring the Contract (Protect Yourself)

When they say yes, the legal structure is everything. If you don't protect your IP and revenue share, the station might later take the code, hire a cheap freelancer, and cut you out.

**Crucial Contractual Clauses:**
*   **New Revenue Only:** Clearly define that you only share revenue generated *digitally* (MoMo subs, DAI, ticketing). You get 0% of their traditional FM broadcast revenue. This makes the station feel safe.
*   **Exclusivity:** You are the *exclusive* digital monetization and tech partner for Pluzz FM. They cannot run a competing app or MoMo subscription service simultaneously.
*   **IP Ownership:** You (the IT company) own the source code and the technology infrastructure. The station only owns their brand and content. You are licensing the tech to them via the revenue share. *This is vital.* If the partnership ends, they lose the tech, and you take your engine elsewhere.
*   **Marketing Commitment:** The station *must* commit to a minimum number of on-air promotions per day (e.g., 10 mentions of the app daily). If they don't drive traffic, the tech is useless.

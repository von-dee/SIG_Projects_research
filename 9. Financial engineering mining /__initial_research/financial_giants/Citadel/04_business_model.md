# Citadel — Business Model

## Overview

Citadel operates a unique **dual-structure business model** that combines a multi-strategy hedge fund (Citadel LLC) with a technology-driven market maker (Citadel Securities). This structure, pioneered by Ken Griffin, creates synergies in technology, research, and talent while diversifying revenue streams and risk profiles.

## Unique Business Model Characteristics

### 1. Pod Shop Model

**Structure**:
- **Semi-autonomous teams**: 4–5 specialists per pod
- **Alpha target**: 2–3% pure, market-neutral alpha
- **Leverage**: ~5x to deliver 10–15% fund-level returns
- **Capital allocation**: Dynamic based on performance
- **Compensation**: Percentage of P&L generated
- **Performance review**: Two consecutive years of underperformance = team dissolution

**Advantages**:
- **Diversification**: Dozens of independent alpha sources
- **Reduced key person risk**: No single team dominates
- **Competition**: Internal competition drives performance
- **Scalability**: Can add/remove pods based on opportunity

### 2. Dual Structure Synergies

| Synergy Area | Citadel LLC | Citadel Securities | Combined Benefit |
|-------------|-------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Technology** | Research infrastructure | Low-latency execution | Shared development costs |
| **Talent** | Researchers, PMs | Engineers, traders | Cross-pollination |
| **Data** | Fundamental research | Market microstructure | Comprehensive data advantage |
| **Risk Management** | Portfolio risk | Execution risk | Unified risk framework |
| **Capital** | LP capital | Trading capital | Efficient capital deployment |

### 3. Capital Discipline

**Philosophy**: Return capital rather than grow beyond optimal size.

**Rationale**:
- **Alpha degradation**: Larger AUM makes alpha generation harder
- **Capacity constraints**: Strategies have limited scalability
- **Investor returns**: Better to return profits than degrade performance
- **Fee optimization**: Performance fees on higher returns > management fees on larger AUM

**Implementation**:
- **Regular distributions**: $32B returned since 2017
- **Lock-up structures**: Minimum two-year lockup (since 1998)
- **Transparent communication**: Clear rationale for returns

### 4. Risk Management Infrastructure

**Proprietary Systems**:
- **Real-time correlation monitoring**: Across all pods and asset classes
- **Position sizing algorithms**: Dynamic based on risk metrics
- **Stress testing**: 10,000+ scenarios daily
- **Liquidity risk assessment**: Real-time monitoring

**Risk Metrics**:
| Metric | Target | Current |
|--------|--------|---------|
| **Portfolio VaR** | <2% monthly | ~1.5% |
| **Maximum Drawdown** | <10% annually | ~8% |
| **Sharpe Ratio** | >1.5 | ~1.8 |
| **Beta to S&P 500** | <0.3 | ~0.2 |

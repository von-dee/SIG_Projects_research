# Two Sigma — Executive Summary

## Overview

Two Sigma Investments is a quantitative hedge fund that describes itself as a "technology company that does finance." Founded in 2001 by David Siegel and John Overdeck, both veterans of computer science and mathematics, the firm has grown to become one of the largest and most technologically advanced quantitative investment managers in the world. Despite internal governance challenges and a high-profile compliance scandal in recent years, Two Sigma achieved record assets under management of approximately **$70 billion** in 2025.

## Core Philosophy

Two Sigma's philosophy is built on three pillars:

1. **Data is Alpha**: Alternative data sources provide information advantage
2. **Machine Learning Scales**: AI can process more data than human analysts
3. **Technology Enables Everything**: Infrastructure is the competitive moat

## Strategic Position

Two Sigma occupies a unique position as a **pure technology-driven quant fund** that heavily invests in alternative data and machine learning. The firm has diversified into:
- Traditional quantitative strategies (equities, fixed income)
- Alternative data integration (satellite imagery, shipping manifests)
- Venture capital and private investments
- Insurance and reinsurance (Two Sigma Insurance)

## Competitive Moat

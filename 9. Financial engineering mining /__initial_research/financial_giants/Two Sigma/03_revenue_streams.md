# Two Sigma — Revenue Streams

## Revenue Structure Overview

Two Sigma operates a traditional hedge fund fee structure, generating revenue through management fees and performance fees. Unlike market makers like Jane Street or Citadel Securities, Two Sigma does not generate trading revenue directly; instead, it charges investors for managing their capital.

## Primary Revenue Streams

### 1. Management Fees

**Description**: Annual fee charged on assets under management, regardless of performance.

| Fund Type | Management Fee | Estimated AUM | Annual Revenue |
|-----------|---------------|--------------|----------------|
| **Flagship Funds** | 1.5%–2.0% | ~$40B | ~$700M–$800M |
| **Institutional Funds** | 1.0%–1.5% | ~$20B | ~$250M–$300M |
| **Risk Premia / Smart Beta** | 0.5%–1.0% | ~$10B | ~$75M–$100M |
| **Total Management Fees** | | **~$70B** | **~$1.0–$1.2B** |

**Description**: Percentage of profits generated by the funds.

## Revenue Per Employee

With ~2,000 employees and ~$3.0B in total revenue:
- **Revenue per employee: ~$1.5M**
- This is strong but below Jane Street (~$11.3M) and Renaissance Technologies (~$12–14M)
- Reflects the asset management model (fee-based) vs. trading model (profit-based)
